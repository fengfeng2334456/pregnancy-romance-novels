## Chapter 7: Prince's Wrath

Before Su Nian could figure out what to do, trouble came looking for her.

That afternoon, she was sitting in the courtyard, basking in the sun, when Xiao Yu appeared without warning.

His face was dark as a thundercloud — the look of a sky before a violent storm.

Su Nian's heart skipped a beat. "Your Highness? What is it?"

Xiao Yu said nothing. He simply waved his hand, dismissing the servants.

Xiao Cui hesitated, then tactfully withdrew.

They were alone in the courtyard.

Xiao Yu stared at Su Nian for a long moment before speaking. "I am going to ask you one question. Answer me truthfully."

Su Nian felt a prickle of unease. "Please go ahead, Your Highness."

"You have been unwell lately. What is the matter?"

Su Nian's heart tightened. How did he know?

"It is nothing serious — just some mild discomfort."

Xiao Yu stepped closer. "You saw a physician? What did the physician say?"

Su Nian took a step back, uncertain whether she should tell the truth.

When she did not answer, a complex emotion flickered in Xiao Yu's eyes. "I have already spoken with the physician."

Su Nian's face drained of color.

Xiao Yu drew a slow breath. His voice was ice. "You are pregnant."

It was not a question. It was a statement.

Su Nian knew she could hide it no longer. She nodded. "Yes."

The words had barely left her lips when a sharp crack rang out — his palm connected with her cheek.

The force of the blow sent her staggering. She nearly fell.

Su Nian clutched her face and stared at Xiao Yu in disbelief.

He had struck her?

Xiao Yu's hand still hung in the air. He seemed momentarily surprised by his own action, but his expression quickly hardened back into cold indifference.

"Tell me — whose child is this?"

Su Nian was stunned.

He was asking whose child it was?

Did he really think...

"Your Highness," she forced the words out, her voice breaking. "The child is yours."

Xiao Yu gave a cold laugh. "Mine? You presume rather a lot. I am the Prince Yu — how could I ever have been involved with a woman of unknown origins like you?"

Su Nian felt as though a blade had been driven through her heart.

"You think I don't know?" Xiao Yu's voice was tight with suppressed fury. "That night, when I was caring for you, you took advantage and threw yourself at me! I thought you were a respectable woman. I had no idea you were so shameless!"

Su Nian's face went deathly pale.

That night... she had been delirious with fever, and Xiao Yu had been a willing participant. How could he say such things?

"Your Highness, you misunderstand. That night, I was —"

"Enough!" Xiao Yu cut her off. "Did you think I would believe your lies? You merely want to use this child to elevate your position! But you have miscalculated!"

Su Nian looked at him. Her eyes grew red.

Was this the man she had fallen for?

She had believed that beneath his cold exterior, he was a good person. She had believed that though he spoke little, he cared for her in his heart.

And the truth?

He did not believe a single word she said. From beginning to end, he had regarded her as nothing more than a scheming woman trying to attach herself to a powerful man.

"You think I don't know?" Xiao Yu sneered. "Your body has issues — your monthly courses are irregular, and you are not easily able to conceive. You used this opportunity to entrap me and marry into the residence, didn't you?"

Su Nian froze.

Her body had issues? This body... was not easily able to conceive?

No — she did not know the original owner's medical history. In her own body, her periods had always been perfectly regular.

"The physician has examined you," Xiao Yu's voice was glacial. "You have a congenital cold constitution, which makes conception difficult. This pregnancy — you must have used some folk remedy to bring it about. Am I right?"

Su Nian opened her mouth to speak, but found she had no defense.

She had only been in this body for two months. Her understanding of its medical history was limited. She did not even know whether the original owner had any underlying conditions.

But how could Xiao Yu think this of her?

He was her savior, and she did have feelings for him. But she had never once entertained the thought of using a child to climb the social ladder!

"Your Highness," her voice was hoarse. "The child is truly yours. If you don't believe me, we can perform a blood test."

"A blood test?" Xiao Yu laughed coldly. "You think I would accept a child of unknown parentage?"

Su Nian's heart went completely cold.

Did that mean... he did not want this child?

"I will give you a sum of silver. Leave the residence, and from this day forward, we will have nothing more to do with each other."

Xiao Yu's words were like a bucket of icy water, drenching her to the bone.

Su Nian stared at him, and the tears she had been holding back finally spilled over.

"Your Highness, how can you say that?" Her voice cracked with sobs. "The child is innocent! Even if you don't want me, you cannot abandon the child!"

A complex emotion flashed through Xiao Yu's eyes, but it was swiftly extinguished by coldness.

"My bloodline will not be left to wander the world," he said. "I will keep this child. But you — you must leave."

Su Nian was stunned.

Keep the child, but send the mother away?

What kind of logic was that?

She was the child's mother. How could he tear them apart?

"No," Su Nian shook her head. "I won't go. I will raise this child myself."

Xiao Yu frowned. "You are a lone woman with nothing. How will you raise a child?"

"I will find a way." Su Nian wiped her tears. "No matter how hard it gets, I will never abandon my child."

Xiao Yu stared at her for a long time. A faint, almost imperceptible emotion stirred in his eyes.

At last, he turned away.

At the doorway, he paused. "In three days, you will leave the residence. I will arrange for someone to escort you."

With that, he strode away without looking back.

Su Nian watched his retreating figure, tears streaming down her face.

This was the man she had fallen in love with.

Cold-blooded. Selfish. He did not care about her feelings at all.

She had been blind to have ever loved him.

But the child...

She pressed a hand to her belly, and the tears fell harder.

I'm sorry, little one. Your mother has failed you.

Your father does not want you. But your mother will love you with all her heart.
