## Chapter 1: Accidental Transmigration

*"It hurts..."*

That was Su Nian's first sensation upon regaining consciousness.

Her head throbbed as though it had been struck with a hammer — a dull, relentless ache. She tried to reach up and touch it, but her arms were so weak she could barely lift them.

What was going on?

She clearly remembered working overtime on a proposal. It had been two in the morning, and she was still at the office, staring at her computer screen. Then her vision had suddenly gone black, and she knew nothing more.

Had she died from overwork?

No — the pain was too real. This wasn't a dream.

Su Nian forced her eyes open. What greeted her was an unfamiliar scene of classical antiquity.

A carved wooden bed, pale blue gauze curtains, and the faint scent of sandalwood in the air. She lay on a hard bed, covered by a thin quilt embroidered with mandarin ducks.

Where was this? A hospital? No — what hospital looked like this?

She struggled to sit up and glanced around. The room was not large, but it was elegantly furnished. On the dressing table sat a bronze mirror and an array of bottles and jars she could not identify. The windows were made of paper, and beyond them she could faintly make out the silhouettes of pavilions and towers.

"What... what is going on?"

Su Nian was utterly bewildered.

She was an ordinary white-collar worker of the twenty-first century. She lived in Beijing's Chaoyang District, worked overtime from nine to six every day, and led a life that ran in a straight line between home and the office. How could she wake up in a place that belonged in a period drama?

Was this a prank by her friends?

No — none of her friends would do something so pointless. Besides, the textures, the smells, the quality of light... everything was far too real.

"Miss, you're awake?"

A crisp voice came from the doorway.

Su Nian turned to look and saw a young girl in ancient dress walking in, carrying a bowl of medicine. The girl looked about fifteen or sixteen, with a round face and bright almond eyes — lively and endearing.

Ancient dress? A film set?

Su Nian grew even more confused.

"Don't move, Miss. Drink your medicine first." The little maid hurried to the bedside and carefully helped her sit up. "The physician said you injured your head and need plenty of rest."

"I..." Su Nian opened her mouth, but her voice came out hoarse and raw. "Where... where am I?"

The maid's face filled with surprise. "Miss, you don't remember? This is Prince Yu's Residence! Three days ago, you were found in a ruined temple outside the city, covered in wounds and unconscious. His Highness was passing by and brought you back to be treated."

Prince Yu's Residence? His Highness the Prince?

Su Nian felt her mind spiral into chaos.

"I... what is my name?" she asked tentatively.

The maid froze, then her eyes reddened. "Miss, what's wrong? Your name is Su Nian! Even if you don't remember, Xiao Cui will always remember your name! You're a good person — if it weren't for you saving me that day, those slave traders would have beaten me to death..."

As she spoke, the maid began wiping away tears.

Su Nian... Su Nian. The name was the same as hers.

Wait — what was actually happening here?

She took a deep breath and forced herself to stay calm. "That... Xiao Cui, was it? Can you tell me — what dynasty is this?"

The maid blinked in confusion. "Dynasty? What do you mean, Miss?"

"I mean — what is the current era? Who is the emperor?"

"It's the twentieth year of Tianyou. The current emperor is the third son of the late emperor..." The maid paused mid-sentence, then it dawned on her. "Miss, how could you not remember these things? Could you have lost your memory?"

Su Nian did not answer, for she was already in complete shock.

The twentieth year of Tianyou? The late emperor? The third prince?

This was... the ancient world? She was in the ancient world?

Su Nian looked down at her own hands — slender, pale, with neatly trimmed nails, but a thin layer of calluses on the palms. These were not her hands. Her own hands were pale too, but her palms had been smooth.

She threw back the quilt and looked down —

This was not her body.

She had been a twenty-eight-year-old urban office worker, one hundred and sixty-five centimeters tall, barely over fifty kilograms. But the body before her was small and slight — she guessed it wasn't even one hundred and sixty centimeters — and was clearly still developing.

"Heavens, is this some kind of joke?"

Su Nian murmured to herself, feeling as though the sky were falling.

She had transmigrated! She had actually transmigrated!

And not just to any ancient dynasty, but to a strange and unfamiliar one, in the body of a girl whose identity she did not know.

This was beyond absurd.

"Miss? Miss, what's wrong?" Xiao Cui saw her face turn deathly pale and rushed forward to steady her. "Miss, please don't scare Xiao Cui!"

Su Nian took a deep breath and forced herself to calm down.

No — she could not panic. In times like these, calm was everything.

Since she had already transmigrated, she had to accept reality. The most important thing now was to figure out the identity of this body's original owner, and why she had been injured.

"Xiao Cui," she said, lowering her voice and trying to keep her tone steady. "Tell me — what did I do before? Why was I injured?"

Xiao Cui dried her tears and answered earnestly. "Before, Miss was the daughter of a farming family outside the city. Your parents died when you were young, and you lived all alone. Three days ago, Xiao Cui was being chased by slave traders and hid in a ruined temple — that was where I met you. You saved me and took me in. But then..."

She paused, and her voice dropped. "Then a group of men in black came to kill you. You were trying to protect me, and they knocked you unconscious. If His Highness hadn't been passing by, you might have..."

At this, Xiao Cui's eyes reddened again.

Su Nian frowned.

The original owner of this body had been hunted? Why? She was a farmer's daughter — she shouldn't have had any enemies.

Could this body be hiding some secret?

"What happened to those men in black? Were they caught?"

"I don't know. His Highness sent people to investigate, but they didn't seem to find anything."

Su Nian fell silent, a vague unease stirring within her.

It seemed this body's identity was not so simple after all. She would have to tread carefully and reveal no flaws.

"Xiao Cui, I've lost my memory — I can't remember anything. Can you tell me about the situation here? Especially about Prince Yu... what kind of person is he?"

At this, Xiao Cui's eyes lit up. "His Highness? He's the God of War of the Great Zhou Dynasty! Young as he is, his military exploits are legendary — the people of Northern Di tremble at his name! There's no one in the entire Capital City who doesn't fear him!"

She lowered her voice. "Though His Highness is a bit cold and doesn't talk much. But he's a good man! If it weren't for him saving you, Miss, you'd have lost your life. Xiao Cui thinks he's the very best person in the whole world!"

Su Nian listened and felt a measure of relief.

It sounded as though Prince Yu was a decent figure — at least, he wouldn't kill innocent people without cause. Since she was currently within his residence, she should be safe for now.

But she still needed to find out the true identity of this body, and why those men in black had been pursuing her.

And then there was the larger question — what was this world, exactly? Could she ever go back?

A cascade of questions swirled in her mind.

But for now, all she could do was recover from her injuries and figure out the rest later.

"Xiao Cui, give me the medicine." She held out her hand.

Xiao Cui hurriedly passed her the bowl. "Drink it while it's warm, Miss."

Su Nian took the bowl, drew a deep breath, and downed it in one go.

Bitter! So bitter she couldn't help grimacing!

But she didn't complain. She simply swallowed it in silence.

This was her first bowl of medicine in the ancient world.

There would likely be many more bitter days ahead.

After the medicine, Xiao Cui brought a bowl of congee. Su Nian was ravenous and finished it in a few hasty mouthfuls.

With food in her stomach, she felt some strength return.

"Where is His Highness now? I'd like to thank him in person."

"His Highness is very busy and rarely at the residence," Xiao Cui said. "But he gave orders that you are to rest and recover here. Once you're well, you can decide what to do next."

Su Nian nodded, though her mind was already calculating.

It seemed she would be staying at Prince Yu's Residence for the foreseeable future. A transmigrator in an unfamiliar ancient world, with no identification and no way to feed herself — the residence was, at the very least, a safe haven.

As for what came after... she would have to take things one step at a time.
