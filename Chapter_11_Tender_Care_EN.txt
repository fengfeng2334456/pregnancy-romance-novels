## Chapter 11: Tender Care

When Su Nian awoke, she found herself lying on an unfamiliar bed.

Calming incense burned in the room, and the faint scent of medicine lingered in the air.

She turned her head and saw Xiao Yu sitting by the bedside, holding a bowl of medicine.

His expression was grim, and there was an unmistakable weariness and worry between his brows.

"Your Highness?" Su Nian stared at him in confusion. "Why are you here?"

Xiao Yu did not answer. He simply brought the medicine bowl to her lips. "Drink."

Su Nian opened her mouth to say something, but Xiao Yu tipped the bowl without giving her a chance to refuse.

Bitter! So bitter she couldn't help but grimace!

But she gritted her teeth and drank it all.

Xiao Yu set down the bowl, his voice low. "The physician said your body is weak and you have been overwrought with worry. You nearly miscarried."

Su Nian's face changed.

Nearly miscarried?

Her baby... nearly lost?

"Fortunately, you were treated in time," Xiao Yu continued. "The child is safe. But you must stay in bed and rest. You are not to wander about."

Su Nian looked at him, unsure of what to say.

Hadn't he just said he wanted her to leave? So why now...

"Your Highness," she ventured carefully, "didn't you say I should leave in three days?"

Xiao Yu fell silent.

After a long while, he spoke. "In your current condition, you cannot leave."

Su Nian gave a bitter smile.

Of course.

He was not keeping her because he was worried about her — only because of the child in her womb.

"Please do not worry, Your Highness," she said. "Once I am a little better, I will leave immediately. I will not cause you any more trouble."

A barely perceptible emotion flickered in Xiao Yu's eyes.

"Do you want to leave that badly?"

Su Nian was taken aback.

Wasn't he the one who told her to go? Why was he asking her?

"Your Highness," she said carefully, "I do not wish to be a burden to you. If you want me to go, I will go."

Xiao Yu's gaze darkened.

He stood up abruptly and walked to the window, turning his back to her.

"Su Nian," his voice was low, "have you ever thought about what will happen to you if you leave?"

Su Nian fell silent.

She had thought about it.

An unmarried woman, carrying a child — in this ancient world, she would have no way to survive.

She had no identification, no money, no connections. Even if she fled to the ends of the earth, she would be found.

Not to mention, she was pregnant and her body was so weak.

She could not leave.

But she could not stay either.

Staying at the residence made her his greatest liability. Consort De, Concubine Liu, the censors — everyone wanted her gone.

"I will take things one step at a time," she said softly. "There will always be a way."

Xiao Yu turned to look at her.

His gaze was bottomless — she could not fathom what he was thinking.

"Do you really have so little faith in me?" he asked.

Su Nian froze. "What do you mean?"

Xiao Yu did not answer. He walked back to the bed and sat down beside her.

He reached out and gently stroked her cheek.

The gesture was so tender it did not seem like him at all.

Su Nian's heart skipped a beat.

"Your Highness..."

"Don't move." Xiao Yu's voice was low. "Let me look at your injury."

Su Nian then remembered — her face had been slapped by Xiao Yu before.

Though several days had passed, there should still be bruising.

Xiao Yu's fingers lightly brushed her cheek, and a flicker of self-reproach crossed his eyes.

"Does it hurt?"

Su Nian shook her head.

It actually hurt a little. But she did not want him to feel guilty.

Xiao Yu sighed and withdrew his hand.

"I'm sorry."

Su Nian froze.

He was apologizing to her?

Xiao Yu — the cold-hearted Prince Yu — was actually apologizing to her?

"I should not have struck you that day," Xiao Yu said. "I was just... too angry."

Su Nian looked at him, unsure of what to say.

Xiao Yu continued, "When the news of your pregnancy reached me, I was truly shocked. I thought you were trying to use the child to elevate your status, so I said many hurtful things."

Tears welled up in Su Nian's eyes again.

She did not want to cry. But she could not help it.

She had endured so many grievances these past days.

Bullied by Liu Ru Yan, framed by Consort De, gossiped about by everyone in the residence...

She had been holding it in, gritting her teeth and pressing on.

But now, a single "I'm sorry" from Xiao Yu caused all her fortitude to crumble.

"Don't cry." Xiao Yu looked at her, at a loss. "I'm hopeless when women cry."

Su Nian laughed through her tears. "Your Highness is so formidable — how could you be afraid of a woman's tears?"

Xiao Yu paused, and the corners of his mouth curved slightly.

It was the first time he had smiled in a long while.

Su Nian looked at his smile, and her heart skipped another beat.

This man truly was handsome when he smiled.

"Rest well," Xiao Yu stood up. "I will come see you tomorrow."

Su Nian nodded. "Take care, Your Highness."

Xiao Yu reached the doorway and suddenly stopped.

"Su Nian."

"Is there anything else, Your Highness?"

Xiao Yu did not turn around. "If anything troubles you in the future, tell me directly. Do not bear it all alone."

Su Nian paused, her eyes reddening again.

"Thank you, Your Highness."

Xiao Yu said no more and strode away.

Su Nian watched his retreating figure, a warmth flooding her heart.

Perhaps... things were not so bad after all.

Perhaps, he did have her in his heart.
