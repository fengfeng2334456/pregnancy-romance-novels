## Chapter 2: Temporary Stay at the Prince's Residence

The following days, Su Nian spent recuperating in bed.

Xiao Cui brought her medicine and meals on time every day, and would also tell her about things in the prince's residence.

Gradually, she gained a preliminary understanding of this world.

The country she was in now was called the Great Zhou Dynasty, and its capital was the capital city. The era she had transmigrated to had no corresponding place in her historical knowledge; apparently, it was a fictional ancient world.

Prince Yu, Xiao Yu, was the younger brother of the current emperor. His fief was in Yu Prefecture on the northwestern frontier. Because of his outstanding military achievements, he was granted the title of "Prince Who Stands Shoulder to Shoulder with the Emperor," second only to the crown prince in status.

But precisely because of this, he had also become a thorn in the side of certain people.

It was said that the emperor both feared and trusted him, and forces in the court were eyeing him covetously. The prince's residence appeared glorious on the surface, but in reality, it was fraught with danger.

After hearing this, Su Nian couldn't help but become more curious about this Prince Yu.

To be able to stand firm in such a treacherous power struggle, this prince was surely not a simple character.

"By the way," she asked Xiao Cui, "what is my current status? How does the prince plan to arrange for me?"

Xiao Cui hesitated: "This... Xiao Cui isn't quite sure either. But the prince said that Miss is a guest and will stay in the residence for a while."

A guest? Su Nian felt somewhat conflicted.

She, a woman of unknown origins, was rescued by the prince and was now living in his residence. If this got out, who knew what gossip would arise.

Moreover, she still needed to figure out why those men in black were hunting her. Who exactly had the original owner of this body offended?

Su Nian lay in bed, her thoughts flying.

In her previous life, she was an orphan, growing up in a welfare institution. After growing up, she worked hard, saved money frugally, and her greatest wish was to buy a house of her own.

As a result, the house wasn't bought, but she transmigrated first.

She didn't know what happened to her body over there. Would her colleagues discover her disappearance? Would the company report it to the police?

Thinking of this, she couldn't help but smile bitterly.

Forget it, thinking so much was useless. Since she had transmigrated, she would live well.

In this life, she wouldn't live as tiredly as she did in her previous life.

She would live for herself once.

On the seventh day of her recovery, Su Nian was finally able to get out of bed and move around.

She had Xiao Cui support her as she walked around the courtyard to familiarize herself with the environment.

The Yu Prince's Residence was very large, covering an area of over a hundred mu. Just the small courtyard where they lived had a front yard, back yard, garden, and pond.

"Miss, this is the courtyard where we live, called Listening to Rain Pavilion." Xiao Cui introduced as they walked, "The prince specially cleaned it up for Miss to live in and even sent maids and old servants to attend to you. Although the courtyard isn't large, it's very quiet."

Su Nian nodded, her gaze scanning the surroundings.

Although the courtyard wasn't large, it was kept very tidy. Several orchids were planted in the corner, and a stone table with a few stone stools was placed under the window. Sunlight filtered through the gaps in the leaves, creating dappled patterns; it was quite pleasant.

If she ignored her current situation, this place seemed like a nice retirement home.

Just as she was thinking, footsteps suddenly came from the courtyard entrance.

Su Nian turned to see a man in dark brocade robes striding in.

The man was tall and straight, probably over 185 cm. His ink-black hair was tied on top of his head, secured with a jade hairpin. His features were deep and cold, with sword-like eyebrows and a high nose bridge, his thin lips pressed tightly together.

He wore a dark patterned brocade robe, a jade belt around his waist, and black boots on his feet. His entire being exuded a chilling aura that made people afraid to look directly at him.

Su Nian's heart skipped a beat.

This should be Prince Yu, Xiao Yu.

Sure enough, just as the rumors said, cold-faced and cold-hearted, just standing there gave people an overwhelming sense of pressure.

Xiao Yu's gaze lingered on her for a moment, seemingly sizing her up.

Su Nian felt a bit uncomfortable under his gaze and subconsciously lowered her eyes.

She didn't know what to say. Although he was her savior, she was a transmigrator after all, knowing nothing about this world. Speaking too much might reveal her flaws, but not speaking would seem impolite.

Just as she was struggling, a deep voice sounded:

"Is the injury better?"

Su Nian was stunned, not expecting him to take the initiative to speak.

"Thank you for saving my life, Your Highness," she curtsied, trying to make her tone sound natural, "this commoner is much better now."

Xiao Yu didn't speak, just looked at her indifferently.

That gaze was like an X-ray, as if trying to see through her.

Su Nian felt nervous under his gaze, not knowing if she had revealed any flaws somewhere.

After a long time, Xiao Yu finally withdrew his gaze: "Since the injury is better, come to the study tomorrow. This prince has some questions to ask you."

With that, he turned and left without giving her any chance to refuse.

Su Nian watched his departing figure, her brow slightly furrowed.

Questions to ask? Ask what?

Did he discover she had lost her memory? Or did he discover something else?

She felt a vague unease in her heart.

"Miss," Xiao Cui leaned over, lowering her voice, "the prince is so scary! Xiao Cui didn't even dare to look at him!"

Su Nian smiled bitterly: "I was also nervous from his gaze."

But speaking of which, this Prince Yu was really good-looking. Although he was cold, his features were exquisite, his temperament outstanding; in modern times, he would definitely be a top-tier abstinence-style male god.

What a pity, he's an ancient person.

Su Nian shook her head, shaking off these messy thoughts.

Now the most important thing was to figure out what Xiao Yu wanted to ask. She had to prepare well and not reveal any flaws.
