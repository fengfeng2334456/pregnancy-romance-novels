## Chapter 20: Prince Chases His Wife

The old woman brought Su Nian back to her home.

Her house was in a small village on the outskirts of the city. Though not wealthy, it was clean and tidy.

"Stay here and rest easy," the old woman said. "We can figure everything out later."

Su Nian was endlessly grateful and thanked her repeatedly.

She settled in at the old woman's home.

The old woman's son and daughter-in-law were ordinary farmers, and they treated her kindly.

Her belly grew larger with each passing day, and the days were relatively peaceful.

But at night, she often thought of Xiao Yu.

She wondered how he was doing now.

She wondered whether he was looking for her.

She wondered how things stood with his marriage to Liu Ru Mei...

She dared not think about it.

Whenever she thought of him, her heart ached as though being cut by a knife.

---

On Xiao Yu's end, he had practically lost his mind.

He had sent everyone he had to search for Su Nian.

Shadow guards, palace guards, household servants — they had practically turned the entire Great Zhou Dynasty upside down.

But Su Nian seemed to have vanished into thin air. No matter how hard they searched, they could not find her.

He could not sleep at night.

Every time he closed his eyes, he saw Su Nian's face.

Her crying, her smiling, her calling his name...

He missed her. He missed her so much it hurt.

He hated himself.

He hated himself for failing to protect her. He hated himself for letting her suffer. He hated himself for not telling her everything sooner.

That day, Liu Ru Yan had told her that His Majesty wanted to send Su Nian away. He had wanted to argue, wanted to tell her the truth.

But before he could say a word, she was gone.

She must have misunderstood.

She must have thought he truly intended to send her away.

And so she left.

Xiao Yu did not know who to blame.

Should he blame Liu Ru Yan for sowing discord? Should he blame Su Nian for not trusting him? Or should he blame himself for being useless?

He only knew one thing — he had to find her.

Even if he had to search every corner of the Great Zhou Dynasty, he would find her.

---

Three months later.

Xiao Yu finally received news of Su Nian.

She was in a small village on the outskirts of the city.

He immediately rode his horse there.

Along the way, his heart was in his throat.

Three months.

Her belly must be quite large by now, right?

How had she managed, alone with such a big belly?

Had she suffered? Had she been wronged?

He dared not think about it.

The thought of what hardships she might have endured made his heart ache as though being cut by a knife.

---

When Xiao Yu arrived at the village, it was already evening.

He dismounted at the village entrance and asked a villager about Su Nian's whereabouts.

"You mean Miss Su?" The villager pointed toward a house not far away. "She lives over there, at Granny Wang's place."

Xiao Yu looked in the direction he pointed and saw a simple thatched cottage with a few vegetables growing in the courtyard.

His Su Nian was living in such a place?

His heart clenched.

He walked quickly over and pushed open the courtyard gate.

In the courtyard, Su Nian was sitting on a small stool, basking in the sun.

She wore a simple homespun garment, her hair simply tied up, her face without any makeup.

But her belly —

Xiao Yu's gaze fell upon her prominently rounded belly, and he froze.

That belly was shockingly large, like a large watermelon, distorting her entire figure.

She supported her waist with one hand and gently stroked her belly with the other, a maternal tenderness on her profile.

But what Xiao Yu saw was only heartache.

She had grown thin.

Except for that big belly, she had lost weight all over. Her cheeks were sunken, her wrists so thin they seemed they might snap with a twist.

How had she endured with such a large belly?

Xiao Yu's eyes reddened.

"Su Nian..." he called softly.

Su Nian heard the voice and turned her head sharply.

When she saw Xiao Yu, she froze.

"Y... Your Highness?" She couldn't believe her eyes.

Xiao Yu walked quickly over and crouched down before her.

He looked at her big belly, his trembling hand reaching out to gently rest upon it.

"Su Nian..." his voice was hoarse. "How... how did you become so thin?"

Su Nian lowered her head, not daring to meet his eyes.

"I... I'm fine..." she bit her lip. "Why has Your Highness come?"

"Why have I come?" Xiao Yu gave a bitter smile. "Su Nian, do you know I've been searching for you for three months?"

Su Nian froze.

"You... you were looking for me?"

"What else?" Xiao Yu looked at her, his eyes slightly red. "Did you think I would let you suffer alone out here?"

His hand gently stroked her big belly, feeling the fetal movement from within.

"He... he's moving..." Xiao Yu said in surprise.

"Yes," Su Nian nodded. "He's been moving a lot lately, especially at night, kicking so much I can't sleep."

Xiao Yu looked at her exhausted appearance, his heart filled with guilt and heartache.

"Su Nian," he took her hand. "I'm sorry... it was my fault... for making you endure all of this alone..."

Su Nian's tears finally couldn't be held back.

"Your Highness..." she choked out. "I thought... I thought you didn't want me anymore..."

"Fool," Xiao Yu pulled her into his arms, gently patting her back. "How could I not want you? You are the person I love most."

He felt the big belly in his embrace and silently swore.

From now on, he would never let her suffer any wrong again.

He would use the rest of his life to make up for the hardships she had endured.

---

Xiao Yu helped Su Nian into the house and had her sit on the bed.

"Let me see," he crouched down and carefully examined her legs. "Are your feet swollen?"

Su Nian nodded embarrassedly. "A little..."

Xiao Yu frowned, looking at her swollen ankles, a sharp pain in his heart.

"Does it hurt?" he pressed gently.

"A little..." Su Nian bit her lip.

Xiao Yu sighed and had someone bring hot water to soak her feet himself.

"Your Highness," Su Nian said hurriedly. "This... this isn't appropriate..."

"What isn't appropriate?" Xiao Yu looked up at her. "You are my woman. It is right for me to care for you."

He gently massaged her feet, his movements tender and careful.

Su Nian looked at his serious expression, and her tears fell again.

"Your Highness," she said softly. "I'm sorry... I shouldn't have misunderstood you... shouldn't have run away alone..."

"It's not your fault," Xiao Yu shook his head. "I didn't explain things clearly to you. From now on, no matter what happens, I will tell you. Don't let your imagination run wild on your own."

Su Nian nodded and leaned against his chest.

At this moment, she felt like the happiest person in the world.

---

Xiao Yu looked at Su Nian's big belly, his heart filled with complex emotions.

He was grateful for this child, for giving him and Su Nian a deeper bond.

But he also felt heartache for Su Nian, for all the suffering she had endured for this child.

"Su Nian," he gently stroked her belly. "You've worked hard."

"It's not hard," Su Nian smiled. "For the baby, everything is worth it."

Xiao Yu lowered his head and kissed her belly gently.

"Little one," he said softly. "You must be good and stop kicking Mother. She has already worked so hard."

A gentle fetal movement came from within, as if responding to him.

Xiao Yu smiled.

"He heard," Su Nian smiled too. "He's greeting you."

Xiao Yu pressed his face against her belly, feeling the life within.

"Su Nian," he said softly. "Thank you."

"Thank me for what?"

"Thank you for being willing to bear my child. Thank you for persisting alone for so long. Thank you... for not giving up."

Su Nian's tears fell again.

"Your Highness," she choked out. "I thank you too... thank you for coming to find me..."

The two embraced each other in this simple thatched cottage, feeling each other's warmth.

---

*(End of Volume Four)*
