## Chapter 15: Clarifying Hearts

After the palace banquet, Xiao Yu brought Su Nian back to Prince Yu's Residence.

The entire way, he held her hand tightly, never letting go for even a moment.

Once they arrived at the residence, he stayed by her side without leaving her for a step.

"Your Highness, you don't need to keep watching over me," Su Nian said, somewhat helpless. "I am fine."

Xiao Yu did not speak. He simply looked at her.

That gaze was so tender it hardly seemed like him — it made her feel somewhat uncomfortable.

"You had a terrible fright today," he said. "Rest early."

Su Nian nodded and was about to lie down when Xiao Yu suddenly spoke:

"Su Nian."

"Hmm?"

Xiao Yu fell silent for a moment, as though carefully choosing his words.

After a long while, he spoke. "At the palace today, you said the person you care about is me. Were you... serious?"

Su Nian's face flushed.

She had not expected him to ask this question at such a moment.

"Didn't you hear me?" she said softly.

"I want to hear it again." Xiao Yu looked at her. "Tell me yourself."

Su Nian took a deep breath, lifted her head, and met his gaze directly.

"Xiao Yu, I like you," she said. "I mean it."

A glimmer of joy flashed in Xiao Yu's eyes.

He did not say a word. He simply leaned down and kissed her gently.

It was a tender, lingering kiss.

Su Nian closed her eyes, feeling the warmth of his lips.

She had never imagined that one day she would kiss this cold-faced prince.

When the kiss ended, Xiao Yu pulled back, resting his forehead against hers.

"Su Nian," his voice was low and gentle, "I like you too."

Su Nian's heart skipped a beat.

He said... he liked her too?

Xiao Yu looked at her stunned expression, the corners of his mouth curving slightly.

"What? Are you surprised that I said I like you?"

Su Nian nodded, then shook her head.

"I thought you... you didn't like me," she said honestly. "You were always so cold to me, and you said you wanted me to leave..."

Xiao Yu sighed. "I had no choice. I am a prince — there are many things I cannot do as I please."

"I know." Su Nian said. "So I don't blame you."

Xiao Yu looked at her, a flicker of guilt in his eyes.

"I'm sorry," he said. "I have made you suffer."

Su Nian shook her head. "You don't need to apologize. As long as you like me, that is enough."

Xiao Yu pulled her into his arms and gently stroked her hair.

"From now on, I will never let you suffer again."

Su Nian leaned against his chest, her heart warm.

She had finally waited for this day.

Xiao Yu liked her.

And she liked him.

Though there were still many obstacles between them, at least for now, their hearts were together.

---

Deep night fell. Su Nian lay in bed, but sleep would not come.

Too much had happened today. She needed time to process it all.

Just then, the door was gently pushed open, and Xiao Yu walked in.

"Your Highness?" Su Nian propped herself up. "Why aren't you asleep yet?"

"I'm worried about you." Xiao Yu sat on the edge of the bed. "Did today frighten you?"

Su Nian nodded. "A little."

Xiao Yu reached out and gently stroked her cheek. "This will never happen again. I promise."

Su Nian looked at him, warmth flooding her heart.

She unconsciously touched her belly. A little life was growing in there.

Xiao Yu noticed her gesture and his gaze fell upon her protruding belly.

"Does your belly still hurt?" he asked.

"It's okay, just a bit heavy." Su Nian smiled. "The baby is getting bigger. It's becoming difficult to walk."

Xiao Yu was silent for a moment, then suddenly reached out and gently covered her round belly with his hand.

Su Nian stiffened all over.

"Your Highness..."

"Don't move." Xiao Yu said softly. "Let me feel."

His palm was warm and broad, covering her belly. When she felt his touch, her heartbeat quickened.

"Did it move today?" Xiao Yu asked.

"It did." Su Nian nodded. "Just a while ago, it kicked me."

As she spoke, a gentle movement came from her belly.

Xiao Yu's eyes lit up. "I felt it!"

He carefully cradled her belly, feeling those faint yet real fetal movements. His gaze was tender — like he was cradling the most precious treasure in the world.

"Su Nian," he suddenly spoke, his voice low and serious. "I want to marry you."

Su Nian froze.

"Not because of the baby in your belly," Xiao Yu continued. "But because I like you. I like you so much I want to spend the rest of my life with you."

Su Nian's eyes grew moist.

"But..." she said, her voice choking. "Your status..."

"I don't care." Xiao Yu cut her off. "I am a prince. I will marry whoever I wish. Whoever dares to oppose will be destroyed."

Su Nian looked at him, tears finally spilling over.

"Idiot, why are you crying?" Xiao Yu gently wiped her tears. "I said I like you. Why are you crying?"

"I... I'm happy..." Su Nian sobbed.

Xiao Yu laughed. It was the gentlest smile she had ever seen.

He leaned down and gently kissed her forehead.

"Su Nian," he said softly. "I will protect you and our child."

His hand remained on her belly, gently stroking.

"When the baby is born, I will teach him to read and practice martial arts, so he becomes the most outstanding person."

"What if it's a daughter?" Su Nian asked.

"That's even better." Xiao Yu's lips curved. "I will spoil her rotten. She will become the happiest little princess."

Su Nian leaned against his chest, feeling his heartbeat.

In that moment, she felt that everything was worth it.

She had finally waited for this day.

Xiao Yu liked her.

And she liked him.

They also had a child together.

Though there would be many unknown challenges ahead, as long as they were together, nothing was to be feared.

"Xiao Yu," she called softly.

"Hmm?"

"I like you too," she said. "I like you so much."

Xiao Yu lowered his head and gently kissed her lips.

"I know."

---

*(End of Volume Three)*
