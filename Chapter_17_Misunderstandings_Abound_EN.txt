## Chapter 17: Misunderstandings Abound

The matter of the commanded marriage dragged on for days.

Xiao Yu was busy from dawn to dusk — either being summoned to the palace to see the Emperor or handling official duties.

Su Nian stayed alone in her courtyard, her heart in turmoil.

She did not know whether Xiao Yu could persuade the Emperor, or what would happen next.

That day, Liu Ru Yan suddenly appeared.

"Miss Su," she smiled with hidden meaning. "It has been a while."

Su Nian looked at her, her guard going up.

Liu Ru Yan had been confined for quite some time — how was she out already?

"What brings Concubine Liu to my humble quarters?" she asked coolly.

Liu Ru Yan covered her mouth with a delicate laugh. "I came to see you, of course. After all, you are His Highness's favorite now."

Su Nian frowned. "Say what you have to say, Concubine."

Liu Ru Yan's smile deepened. "Miss Su is as straightforward as ever. Very well — I will not beat around the bush."

She leaned closer to Su Nian and lowered her voice. "Miss Su, did you know that my elder sister is to marry into the residence?"

Su Nian's face changed.

Liu Ru Yan continued, "His Majesty commanded the marriage, and His Highness defied the decree. His Majesty was already furious. But later, I heard that His Highness agreed to a condition, and His Majesty reluctantly agreed to postpone the wedding."

Su Nian's heart tightened. "What condition?"

Liu Ru Yan smiled. "His Highness agreed... to send Miss Su out of the Capital City."

Su Nian froze.

What?

Xiao Yu had agreed to send her away?

"Miss Su," Liu Ru Yan looked at her, a mocking gleam in her eyes. "Did you truly believe His Highness cared for you? He was merely infatuated with something new. Once my sister enters the household, he will naturally realize who is best suited for him."

Su Nian's face went deathly pale.

She did not believe it. She did not believe Xiao Yu would do such a thing.

But... if it were not true, how would Liu Ru Yan know?

"No... impossible," she murmured.

Liu Ru Yan gave a cold smile. "Believe it or not. But I heard that His Highness has already made the arrangements — you will be sent out of the Capital City tomorrow."

With that, she turned and left, leaving Su Nian standing there in stunned silence.

Su Nian sat alone in her room, her mind in chaos.

Xiao Yu had agreed to send her away?

Was it true?

She wanted to go find Xiao Yu and demand the truth, but she did not dare.

What if it was real?

What if he truly did want to send her away?

She did not know what to do.

The matter of the commanded marriage dragged on for days.

Xiao Yu was busy from dawn to dusk — either being summoned to the palace to see the Emperor or handling official duties.

Su Nian stayed alone in her courtyard, her heart in turmoil.

She did not know whether Xiao Yu could persuade the Emperor, or what would happen next.

That evening, Su Nian sat alone in her room.

Her belly was already over six months along, protruding prominently like a large watermelon. She lowered her head to look at her belly and couldn't help but reach out to gently caress it.

"Little one," she murmured. "Your father has been very busy lately and couldn't come to see us. Please don't blame him, okay?"

A gentle fetal movement came from within, as if the baby was responding to her.

Su Nian smiled, though her eyes grew moist.

She struggled to stand, one hand supporting her waist, the other cradling her belly, and paced slowly around the room. Sitting too long caused back pain, standing too long caused leg swelling. The experience of pregnancy was truly difficult.

She walked to the window and gazed at the moonlit night.

The moonlight was like water, spilling onto her protruding belly, creating a gentle glow.

"If only I could go outside for a walk," she sighed. "But this belly..."

She couldn't even bend over now, let alone go out. Every time she wanted to get up, she needed a maid's help. Even walking required careful caution, for fear of losing balance and falling.

She slowly walked back to the bed and sat down with difficulty.

The bed was soft, but she simply couldn't sleep. Her big belly pressed on her, making it hard to breathe. She could only lie on her side, with a pillow behind her waist for support.

"Little one," she gently patted her belly. "Can't you sleep either?"

Another fetal movement came, this time more obvious. The little one was kicking and thrashing inside, as if protesting something.

"All right, all right," Su Nian gently soothed. "Mother knows you're uncomfortable. Hold on a little longer. In a few months, you can come out."

She closed her eyes, but tears slipped from the corners.

She missed Xiao Yu.

She missed his embrace, his tenderness, his deep voice when he said "I like you"...

But now, she could only sit alone guarding this big belly, suffering through the long nights in solitude.

---

Deep into the night, Su Nian still could not sleep.

She struggled to turn over, her big belly making every movement difficult. She lay on her side, one hand propping herself up on the bed, the other gently stroking her belly.

"Little one," she whispered. "Mother only has you now. No matter what happens, Mother will protect you."

Moonlight streamed through the window lattice, illuminating her prominently rounded belly.

She looked at her belly, her heart filled with mixed emotions.

This big belly had once been her happiest proof of love. But now, it had become a burden.

Even escaping had become difficult for her.

If she were truly sent away, where could she go with such a large belly?

She dared not think about it.
