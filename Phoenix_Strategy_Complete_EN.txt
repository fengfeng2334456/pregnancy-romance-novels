# *Phoenix's Scheme: The Transmigrated Princess Consort Runs Away with Child*

## Synopsis

A modern girl named Su Nian accidentally transmigrates to ancient times, becoming a helpless orphan girl. Through a twist of fate, she is rescued by the cold-faced Prince Xiao Yu — but one reckless night leaves her secretly carrying his child.

One is a soul from the future; the other, a prince who wields immense power over the court. When the truth of her pregnancy is revealed, a delicate emotion begins to blossom between them. Yet the gulf in their stations, layers of misunderstanding, political conspiracies, and the schemes of a rival in love threaten to tear them apart...

She longs to flee but cannot escape the bonds of destiny. He wishes her to stay but knows not how to make her remain.

A love that spans a thousand years — a tale of anguish and devotion, of marriage before love.

She ran away carrying his child; now he must chase his wife through a crucible of regret.

---

## Main Characters

### Female Lead: Su Nian
- A transmigrator from the modern era, formerly an ordinary white-collar worker of the 21st century
- Personality: intelligent and independent, resilient and forbearing, with a tender heart
- Skills: knowledgeable in medicine, an excellent cook, and equipped with a modern mindset
- Fate: plunged into dire straits after an unexpected pregnancy

### Male Lead: Xiao Yu (Prince Yu)
- A prince of the realm, cold in demeanor and heart, with illustrious military achievements
- Personality: cold on the outside but warm within, commanding yet deeply devoted, awkward at expressing his feelings
- Fate: drawn into a political maelstrom, separated from the female lead through misunderstanding

### Second Male Lead: Gu Yan
- A gentleman of noble lineage, gentle as jade
- Falls in love with the female lead at first sight and becomes her protector
- Identity: son of the Chief Physician of the Imperial Medical Court

### Antagonist: Liu Ru Yan
- A candidate for the position of Concubine in the prince's household
- Deeply scheming, calculating at every step
- Constantly targets the female lead

### Key Supporting Characters
- Mammy Wang: Steward of Prince Yu's Residence; initially makes things difficult for the female lead but is later moved by her sincerity
- Prince Yu's Mother: Consort De, who vehemently opposes the match
- Xiao Cui: The female lead's personal maid, fiercely loyal

# Volume One: Transmigration and First Encounter


## Chapter 1: Accidental Transmigration

*"It hurts..."*

That was Su Nian's first sensation upon regaining consciousness.

Her head throbbed as though it had been struck with a hammer — a dull, relentless ache. She tried to reach up and touch it, but her arms were so weak she could barely lift them.

What was going on?

She clearly remembered working overtime on a proposal. It had been two in the morning, and she was still at the office, staring at her computer screen. Then her vision had suddenly gone black, and she knew nothing more.

Had she died from overwork?

No — the pain was too real. This wasn't a dream.

Su Nian forced her eyes open. What greeted her was an unfamiliar scene of classical antiquity.

A carved wooden bed, pale blue gauze curtains, and the faint scent of sandalwood in the air. She lay on a hard bed, covered by a thin quilt embroidered with mandarin ducks.

Where was this? A hospital? No — what hospital looked like this?

She struggled to sit up and glanced around. The room was not large, but it was elegantly furnished. On the dressing table sat a bronze mirror and an array of bottles and jars she could not identify. The windows were made of paper, and beyond them she could faintly make out the silhouettes of pavilions and towers.

"What... what is going on?"

Su Nian was utterly bewildered.

She was an ordinary white-collar worker of the twenty-first century. She lived in Beijing's Chaoyang District, worked overtime from nine to six every day, and led a life that ran in a straight line between home and the office. How could she wake up in a place that belonged in a period drama?

Was this a prank by her friends?

No — none of her friends would do something so pointless. Besides, the textures, the smells, the quality of light... everything was far too real.

"Miss, you're awake?"

A crisp voice came from the doorway.

Su Nian turned to look and saw a young girl in ancient dress walking in, carrying a bowl of medicine. The girl looked about fifteen or sixteen, with a round face and bright almond eyes — lively and endearing.

Ancient dress? A film set?

Su Nian grew even more confused.

"Don't move, Miss. Drink your medicine first." The little maid hurried to the bedside and carefully helped her sit up. "The physician said you injured your head and need plenty of rest."

"I..." Su Nian opened her mouth, but her voice came out hoarse and raw. "Where... where am I?"

The maid's face filled with surprise. "Miss, you don't remember? This is Prince Yu's Residence! Three days ago, you were found in a ruined temple outside the city, covered in wounds and unconscious. His Highness was passing by and brought you back to be treated."

Prince Yu's Residence? His Highness the Prince?

Su Nian felt her mind spiral into chaos.

"I... what is my name?" she asked tentatively.

The maid froze, then her eyes reddened. "Miss, what's wrong? Your name is Su Nian! Even if you don't remember, Xiao Cui will always remember your name! You're a good person — if it weren't for you saving me that day, those slave traders would have beaten me to death..."

As she spoke, the maid began wiping away tears.

Su Nian... Su Nian. The name was the same as hers.

Wait — what was actually happening here?

She took a deep breath and forced herself to stay calm. "That... Xiao Cui, was it? Can you tell me — what dynasty is this?"

The maid blinked in confusion. "Dynasty? What do you mean, Miss?"

"I mean — what is the current era? Who is the emperor?"

"It's the twentieth year of Tianyou. The current emperor is the third son of the late emperor..." The maid paused mid-sentence, then it dawned on her. "Miss, how could you not remember these things? Could you have lost your memory?"

Su Nian did not answer, for she was already in complete shock.

The twentieth year of Tianyou? The late emperor? The third prince?

This was... the ancient world? She was in the ancient world?

Su Nian looked down at her own hands — slender, pale, with neatly trimmed nails, but a thin layer of calluses on the palms. These were not her hands. Her own hands were pale too, but her palms had been smooth.

She threw back the quilt and looked down —

This was not her body.

She had been a twenty-eight-year-old urban office worker, one hundred and sixty-five centimeters tall, barely over fifty kilograms. But the body before her was small and slight — she guessed it wasn't even one hundred and sixty centimeters — and was clearly still developing.

"Heavens, is this some kind of joke?"

Su Nian murmured to herself, feeling as though the sky were falling.

She had transmigrated! She had actually transmigrated!

And not just to any ancient dynasty, but to a strange and unfamiliar one, in the body of a girl whose identity she did not know.

This was beyond absurd.

"Miss? Miss, what's wrong?" Xiao Cui saw her face turn deathly pale and rushed forward to steady her. "Miss, please don't scare Xiao Cui!"

Su Nian took a deep breath and forced herself to calm down.

No — she could not panic. In times like these, calm was everything.

Since she had already transmigrated, she had to accept reality. The most important thing now was to figure out the identity of this body's original owner, and why she had been injured.

"Xiao Cui," she said, lowering her voice and trying to keep her tone steady. "Tell me — what did I do before? Why was I injured?"

Xiao Cui dried her tears and answered earnestly. "Before, Miss was the daughter of a farming family outside the city. Your parents died when you were young, and you lived all alone. Three days ago, Xiao Cui was being chased by slave traders and hid in a ruined temple — that was where I met you. You saved me and took me in. But then..."

She paused, and her voice dropped. "Then a group of men in black came to kill you. You were trying to protect me, and they knocked you unconscious. If His Highness hadn't been passing by, you might have..."

At this, Xiao Cui's eyes reddened again.

Su Nian frowned.

The original owner of this body had been hunted? Why? She was a farmer's daughter — she shouldn't have had any enemies.

Could this body be hiding some secret?

"What happened to those men in black? Were they caught?"

"I don't know. His Highness sent people to investigate, but they didn't seem to find anything."

Su Nian fell silent, a vague unease stirring within her.

It seemed this body's identity was not so simple after all. She would have to tread carefully and reveal no flaws.

"Xiao Cui, I've lost my memory — I can't remember anything. Can you tell me about the situation here? Especially about Prince Yu... what kind of person is he?"

At this, Xiao Cui's eyes lit up. "His Highness? He's the God of War of the Great Zhou Dynasty! Young as he is, his military exploits are legendary — the people of Northern Di tremble at his name! There's no one in the entire Capital City who doesn't fear him!"

She lowered her voice. "Though His Highness is a bit cold and doesn't talk much. But he's a good man! If it weren't for him saving you, Miss, you'd have lost your life. Xiao Cui thinks he's the very best person in the whole world!"

Su Nian listened and felt a measure of relief.

It sounded as though Prince Yu was a decent figure — at least, he wouldn't kill innocent people without cause. Since she was currently within his residence, she should be safe for now.

But she still needed to find out the true identity of this body, and why those men in black had been pursuing her.

And then there was the larger question — what was this world, exactly? Could she ever go back?

A cascade of questions swirled in her mind.

But for now, all she could do was recover from her injuries and figure out the rest later.

"Xiao Cui, give me the medicine." She held out her hand.

Xiao Cui hurriedly passed her the bowl. "Drink it while it's warm, Miss."

Su Nian took the bowl, drew a deep breath, and downed it in one go.

Bitter! So bitter she couldn't help grimacing!

But she didn't complain. She simply swallowed it in silence.

This was her first bowl of medicine in the ancient world.

There would likely be many more bitter days ahead.

After the medicine, Xiao Cui brought a bowl of congee. Su Nian was ravenous and finished it in a few hasty mouthfuls.

With food in her stomach, she felt some strength return.

"Where is His Highness now? I'd like to thank him in person."

"His Highness is very busy and rarely at the residence," Xiao Cui said. "But he gave orders that you are to rest and recover here. Once you're well, you can decide what to do next."

Su Nian nodded, though her mind was already calculating.

It seemed she would be staying at Prince Yu's Residence for the foreseeable future. A transmigrator in an unfamiliar ancient world, with no identification and no way to feed herself — the residence was, at the very least, a safe haven.

As for what came after... she would have to take things one step at a time.

## Chapter 2: Temporary Stay

Over the following days, Su Nian remained in bed, recovering from her injuries.

Xiao Cui brought her medicine and meals on time each day, and chatted with her about life in the residence.

Gradually, she gained a preliminary understanding of this world.

The country she now found herself in was called the Great Zhou Dynasty, and its capital was the Capital City. The era she had transmigrated into bore no resemblance to any period in her knowledge of history — this was clearly a fictional ancient world.

Prince Yu, Xiao Yu, was the emperor's own younger brother. His fiefdom lay in Yuzhou on the northwestern frontier. For his outstanding military service, he had been granted the title of "Single-Character Equal-Rank Prince," a rank second only to the Crown Prince.

But precisely because of this, he had also become a thorn in certain people's sides.

It was said that the emperor both feared and trusted him, and that the various factions at court watched him like hawks. The prince's residence appeared grand and glorious, but beneath the surface, danger lurked on every side.

When Su Nian heard all this, she could not help feeling a measure of curiosity about Prince Yu.

To stand unshaken in such a treacherous arena of power — this prince must be no ordinary man.

"By the way," she asked Xiao Cui, "what is my current status here? What does the prince plan to do with me?"

Xiao Cui hemmed and hawed. "Well... Xiao Cui isn't entirely sure either. But His Highness said you are a guest, and that you'll be staying at the residence for a while."

A guest? Su Nian's feelings were complicated.

She was a girl of unknown origins, rescued by the prince and now living in his household. If word got out, she couldn't imagine what kind of gossip would spread.

Besides, she still needed to figure out why those men in black had been hunting her. Who had the original owner of this body offended?

Su Nian lay under the quilt, her thoughts racing.

In her previous life, she had been an orphan, raised in a welfare institution. After growing up, she had worked hard, scrimped and saved, and her greatest wish had been to buy a home of her own.

Instead of buying that home, she had transmigrated.

She wondered how her original body was doing. Would her colleagues notice she was missing? Would her company file a police report?

The thought made her smile bitterly.

Enough — dwelling on it was useless. Since she had transmigrated, she would simply have to make the best of this new life.

In this lifetime, she would not live as exhaustingly as she had in her last.

She would live for herself, for once.

On the seventh day of her recovery, Su Nian was finally able to get out of bed.

She had Xiao Cui help her walk around the courtyard so she could familiarize herself with the surroundings.

Prince Yu's Residence was enormous, covering well over a hundred *mu* of land. The small courtyard where they stayed alone had a front yard, a rear yard, a garden, and a lotus pond.

"Miss, this is our courtyard — it's called the Listening Rain Pavilion." Xiao Cui pointed things out as they walked. "His Highness specially had it renovated for you, and even assigned maids and matrons to attend to you. The courtyard isn't large, but it's very peaceful."

Su Nian nodded, her gaze sweeping her surroundings.

The courtyard was not large, but it was immaculately kept. Orchids grew in the corners, and beneath the window sat a stone table with several stone benches. Sunlight filtered through the gaps in the leaves, casting dappled shadows — it was actually rather pleasant.

If she ignored her current predicament, it would make a rather nice place to spend one's later years.

Just then, footsteps sounded at the courtyard gate.

Su Nian turned to look and saw a tall, upright man in a dark brocade robe striding in.

He was over one hundred and eighty-five centimeters tall, his ink-black hair bound at the crown and secured with a jade hairpin. His features were deep and striking — sword-like brows that reached his temples, a high-bridged nose, and thin lips pressed firmly together.

He wore a dark brocade robe with subtle patterns, a jade belt at his waist, and black boots on his feet. His entire being radiated an imposing, frosty aura that made one afraid to meet his gaze.

Su Nian's heart skipped a beat.

This must be Prince Yu — Xiao Yu.

Just as the rumors said: cold in face and cold in heart. Merely standing there, he exuded an overwhelming sense of pressure.

Xiao Yu's gaze lingered on her for a moment, as though appraising her.

Su Nian grew uncomfortable under his scrutiny and instinctively lowered her eyes.

She did not know what to say. Though he was her savior, she was, after all, a transmigrator who knew nothing of this world. Say too much and she risked exposing herself; say nothing and she would seem rude.

Just as she was wrestling with the dilemma, a low voice spoke:

"Are your injuries healing?"

Su Nian was taken aback — she hadn't expected him to initiate conversation.

"Thank you for saving my life, Your Highness." She curtsied, striving to keep her tone natural. "I am much better now."

Xiao Yu said nothing. He simply looked at her, his expression unreadable.

That gaze was like an X-ray, as though he could see right through her.

Su Nian felt a prickle of unease. Had she given something away?

After a long moment, Xiao Yu finally withdrew his gaze. "Since your injuries have improved, come to my study tomorrow. There are some matters I wish to ask you."

With that, he turned and walked away, giving her no opportunity to refuse.

Su Nian frowned slightly as she watched him go.

Matters to ask? About what?

Had he realized she had lost her memory? Or had he noticed something else?

A vague unease settled in her chest.

"Miss," Xiao Cui whispered, leaning close. "His Highness is so scary! Xiao Cui didn't even dare look at him!"

Su Nian gave a wry smile. "He made me nervous too."

But now that she thought about it, Prince Yu really was remarkably handsome. Cold, yes — but with exquisite features and an extraordinary presence. In the modern world, he would have been the textbook definition of an aloof heartthrob.

Pity he was an ancient man.

Su Nian shook her head and banished the wayward thoughts.

The most important thing now was to figure out what Xiao Yu wanted to ask. She needed to prepare carefully and not reveal any cracks in her story.

## Chapter 3: First Confrontation

Early the next morning, Su Nian dressed carefully and followed Xiao Cui to the prince's study.

The study of Prince Yu's Residence was located on the eastern side of the main compound, in an independent courtyard of its own. Two guards stood at the gate; when they saw her approach, they bowed and let her pass.

Su Nian drew a deep breath and stepped inside.

The study was large, its decor simple yet refined. Bookshelves lined three walls, filled with volumes of every description. At the center stood a massive rosewood desk, atop which lay several official documents.

Xiao Yu sat behind the desk, reading a book.

At the sound of her footsteps, he looked up.

"Sit."

Su Nian obeyed, taking the chair at the lower end. She sat with her back straight and her hands folded neatly on her knees.

Xiao Yu set down his book and fixed his gaze upon her.

"Your name is Su Nian?"

"Yes."

"How old are you?"

"...Seventeen." Su Nian answered uncertainly. This was what Xiao Cui had told her.

"Where are you from?"

"The outskirts of the Capital City."

"Do you have any living family?"

"No. My parents died when I was young."

Xiao Yu's gaze flickered. "The people who pursued you that day — do you know who they were?"

Su Nian's heart tightened. She shook her head. "I don't remember."

"You truly don't remember?"

There was a note of significance in Xiao Yu's tone.

Su Nian gritted her teeth and nodded. "I struck my head. Many things are a blank to me."

Xiao Yu stared at her for a long while, making her feel as though she were sitting on pins and needles.

Just as she was about to crack under the pressure, he finally looked away. "Very well. I have already sent men to investigate. Until the matter is cleared up, you will remain at the residence."

Su Nian breathed a sigh of relief. "Thank you, Your Highness."

"Do not thank me." Xiao Yu's tone was indifferent. "I saved you because there is something I want from you."

Su Nian blinked. "What is that?"

Xiao Yu did not answer. Instead, he asked, "Can you read and write?"

"...A little." Su Nian replied cautiously. In her previous life, she had been a university graduate — of course she could read. But she did not know whether the original owner of this body had been literate.

"Then help me copy some ledgers." Xiao Yu rose, pulled several thick volumes from the shelves, and set them before her. "Once you have finished copying them, I will let you leave."

Su Nian looked down. They were hefty account books.

It seemed the prince intended to put her to work as free labor.

Still, this was not entirely unwelcome. At least it gave her a legitimate reason to remain at the residence, and she could use the opportunity to familiarize herself with this world's writing and systems.

"I will do as you command."

Xiao Yu nodded. He was about to say something more when a commotion erupted outside.

"Your Highness! Your Highness!" A sharp female voice drew nearer. "I heard that Your Highness has brought a woman back to the residence — I came to see for myself!"

Su Nian turned to look. A gorgeously dressed woman was sweeping into the study.

She was in her early twenties, strikingly beautiful, with almond eyes that sparkled with vivacity. She wore a pink embroidered gown, her hair adorned with pearls and jade, and swayed gently as she walked — the very picture of allure.

Xiao Yu's brow creased. "Concubine Liu, this is my study. What are you doing here?"

Su Nian understood at once.

So this was the prince's concubine. No wonder she had rushed over the moment she heard he had brought a woman home.

It seemed Prince Yu was rather popular with the ladies.

Liu Ru Yan entered the study and her gaze immediately fell on Su Nian, sweeping over her from head to toe.

"So this is the woman His Highness brought back from outside?" She covered her lips with a delicate smile, her voice laced with contempt. "She doesn't look like much. So thin and small — not pretty at all."

Su Nian felt a flicker of irritation but kept her expression neutral.

She did not know what influence this concubine wielded, and provoking her rashly would be unwise.

"Concubine Liu is too kind." She gave a slight bow. "I am merely staying at the residence to recover from my injuries. I could never compare to you, my lady."

Liu Ru Yan's smile deepened. "At least you know your place."

She turned to Xiao Yu and adopted a coaxing tone. "Your Highness, I heard that this woman's origins are unknown. How can you let someone of unclear background stay in the residence? What if she is a dangerous person..."

"Enough." Xiao Yu cut her off, his voice dropping several degrees. "My affairs are not yours to comment on."

Liu Ru Yan's smile froze. A flash of hurt crossed her eyes.

"Your Highness..."

"Leave." His tone brooked no argument.

Liu Ru Yan bit her lip, shot Su Nian a venomous glare, and turned on her heel.

Su Nian watched her retreating figure, her feelings mixed.

It seemed Concubine Liu genuinely had feelings for Xiao Yu. But the falling blossom harbored longing while the flowing water remained indifferent — Xiao Yu clearly had no feelings for her whatsoever.

"Have you seen enough?" Xiao Yu's voice pulled her back to reality.

Su Nian averted her gaze and lowered her eyes. "I apologize, Your Highness."

Xiao Yu said nothing further. He merely waved his hand. "Take the ledgers and go. Return when you have finished copying them."

Su Nian gathered the thick volumes into her arms, curtsied, and turned to leave.

Only after stepping out of the study did she allow herself a long exhale.

Prince Yu's presence was truly formidable. He had not said a single harsh word, yet she was drenched in cold sweat.

But Concubine Liu was clearly no easy opponent either. Living in the residence, Su Nian would likely face her hostility again and again.

Never mind — she would cross each bridge when she came to it.

For now, the most important thing was to familiarize herself with this world's writing and institutions, and to investigate the origins of those men in black along the way.

## Chapter 4: Budding Affection

In the days that followed, Su Nian spent every day in the study, copying ledgers.

Xiao Cui kept her company, grinding ink and pouring tea.

Gradually, Su Nian gained a working knowledge of this world's writing system.

The script used here resembled traditional Chinese characters — she could recognize most of them, though the writing conventions were somewhat different. Fortunately, she had practiced calligraphy in her previous life and was competent with both pen and brush, so she managed well enough.

As she copied the ledgers, she also absorbed information about the world.

The economic system of the Great Zhou Dynasty, its taxation policies, its commercial trade — all were reflected in the account books.

As she wrote, Su Nian couldn't help but marvel: being a bookkeeper in ancient times was no easy task. So many characters to write, and all with a brush.

One afternoon, she was absorbed in her work when footsteps sounded at the door.

She looked up to find Xiao Yu standing at the entrance, watching her. She had no idea how long he had been there.

"Your Highness?" Su Nian rose hastily and curtsied. "When did you arrive?"

"Just now." Xiao Yu entered the study and picked up one of the finished ledgers to inspect.

Su Nian felt a flutter of nervousness. Would he find her calligraphy inadequate?

After a moment, Xiao Yu set the ledger down. "Your handwriting is better than I expected."

Su Nian relaxed. "You flatter me, Your Highness. I merely took care to be thorough."

Xiao Yu said nothing. His gaze lingered on her face for a moment.

Su Nian grew uncomfortable under his scrutiny and lowered her eyes.

The atmosphere between them turned subtly charged.

Just then, Su Nian's stomach let out a loud growl.

Her face flushed crimson. She wished the ground would open up and swallow her.

She had been so absorbed in copying that she had forgotten to eat lunch. And now she had embarrassed herself in front of the prince!

Xiao Yu raised an eyebrow, a flicker of surprise in his eyes.

"Hungry?"

Su Nian nodded, mortified. "Yes... I forgot to eat my midday meal."

Xiao Yu was silent for a moment, then turned to the guard at the door. "Tell the kitchen to send some refreshments."

"Yes, Your Highness."

Su Nian was taken aback. She hadn't expected him to take the initiative.

A short while later, the guard returned with a plate of delicacies — exquisite osmanthus cakes that gave off an enticing aroma.

"Eat." Xiao Yu's tone was casual.

Su Nian hesitated, then reached for a piece of osmanthus cake.

She took a bite. It was sweet but not cloying, melting on the tongue.

Delicious!

Her eyes brightened, and she couldn't help reaching for another piece.

Xiao Yu watched her eat with evident enjoyment, and a faint, almost imperceptible smile touched his eyes.

"You have a healthy appetite."

Su Nian froze. She looked up and found herself gazing directly into his eyes.

They were very close. So close that she could see the fine patterns in his irises and catch the faint scent of sandalwood that clung to him.

Su Nian's heart skipped a beat.

She quickly looked away. "Your Highness is amusing himself. I was simply hungry."

Xiao Yu said nothing. He simply watched her in silence.

That gaze was bottomless — impossible to fathom what he was thinking.

Su Nian's pulse quickened. She was about to speak when an announcement came from outside:

"Your Highness, Mammy Cui from Consort De's household has arrived!"

Xiao Yu's brow creased. He withdrew his gaze. "Let her in."

A matron in her forties entered and curtsied. "This servant greets Your Highness. Your Highness, Consort De requests that you visit the palace tomorrow. She says there is an important matter to discuss."

"Understood." Xiao Yu replied coolly.

Mammy Cui's gaze lingered on Su Nian for a moment. She hesitated, as though wanting to say something.

Xiao Yu frowned. "Is there something else?"

"Her Ladyship says..." Mammy Cui faltered. "Her Ladyship says that a woman of unknown origins has been brought into the residence, and she urges Your Highness to be cautious. It would be best to send her away as soon as possible, before any gossip spreads."

Su Nian felt a sting at these words.

She knew she was a burden, but being dismissed so openly to her face was still uncomfortable.

Xiao Yu's voice turned cold. "My mother is remarkably well-informed."

Mammy Cui's expression shifted. "Her Ladyship is only concerned for Your Highness..."

"My affairs do not require my mother's attention." Xiao Yu cut her off. "Return and tell Her Ladyship that I will visit the palace tomorrow."

"Yes, Your Highness." Mammy Cui dared not say more. She curtsied and withdrew.

Once again, only Su Nian and Xiao Yu remained in the study.

The atmosphere grew awkward.

Su Nian did not know what to say, so she lowered her head and resumed copying.

But her mind was nowhere near the ledgers.

Consort De was clearly Xiao Yu's mother — not his birth mother, perhaps, but a member of the imperial family nonetheless. For Su Nian to be living in the prince's residence was, admittedly, inappropriate.

"What are you thinking?" Xiao Yu's voice broke the silence.

Su Nian looked up and chose her words carefully. "I was thinking that perhaps I should leave the residence, so as not to cause trouble for Your Highness."

Xiao Yu studied her. "You want to leave?"

Su Nian shook her head. "I have nowhere to go at present, and the people who pursued me have not yet been identified. I would prefer to wait until the truth comes to light before deciding what to do."

Xiao Yu was silent for a moment. "Then stay."

Su Nian was caught off guard. "Your Highness?"

Xiao Yu rose to his feet. "The person I saved is not for others to pass judgment on."

With that, he strode out of the study.

Su Nian watched his retreating figure, a strange and unnameable warmth rising in her chest.

This man — so cold and aloof on the surface — yet he always seemed to step in for her at the crucial moment.

She knew it was wrong. She was a transmigrator; sooner or later, she would have to leave this place.

But in this moment, her heart had stirred, despite herself.

## Chapter 5: Unexpected Intimacy

That night, Su Nian had a strange dream.

She dreamed she was lost in a darkness so thick she could not see her own hands. Someone was chasing her.

"Grab her! Don't let her get away!"

The voice was sinister, sending a chill through her entire body.

She ran with all her might, but her legs felt leaden — she could not move fast enough.

Suddenly, a hand seized her ankle.

She fell. When she looked back, a dark figure was crawling slowly toward her.

"Su Nian... Su Nian..."

The figure let out an eerie laugh. "Did you think you could escape?"

Su Nian jolted awake.

She gasped for breath and found herself drenched in sweat.

Outside, the moonlight was hazy. The night was deep.

Su Nian sat up and pressed a hand to her chest.

Thank goodness — it was only a dream.

She was about to lie back down when she heard a commotion outside.

"What's happening?" She threw on her outer robe and went to the window.

Flames flickered in the courtyard. There seemed to be a great many people about.

Su Nian's heart clenched. She pushed open the door and stepped outside.

Xiao Cui came running toward her, breathless. "Miss, something terrible has happened! There's a fire!"

A fire?

Su Nian's blood ran cold. "Where?"

"The eastern courtyard! Not far from here!" Xiao Cui's face was ashen. "They say someone set it deliberately. The blaze is enormous — several rooms have already burned!"

Su Nian looked out. The distant sky was glowing red, and thick smoke billowed upward.

If the fire continued to spread, the entire residence would be in danger!

"Quick — we have to help put it out!" Su Nian said, and started running.

Xiao Cui grabbed her arm. "Miss, don't go! It's too dangerous!"

"I have to!" Su Nian shook off her hand. "I need to see if there's anything I can do!"

In her previous life, she had been an office worker, but she was not the type to hide behind others. Besides, if the fire reached their courtyard, they would have nowhere to run.

Ignoring Xiao Cui's attempts to stop her, Su Nian sprinted out of the courtyard.

Along the way, she saw many servants hauling water, chaos everywhere.

The fire was indeed massive. Roaring flames devoured the buildings, filling the air with the crackle and pop of burning timber.

Su Nian tried to help, but the smoke choked her until she doubled over coughing.

"Young lady, it's dangerous here — step back!" A guard blocked her path.

Su Nian was about to respond when her foot slipped on something and she pitched backward —

Just as she thought she was about to fall, a hand caught her in time.

"Careful."

The voice was deep and pleasing, with a familiar quality.

Su Nian looked up and met Xiao Yu's gaze.

He was dressed in black martial attire tonight, his posture as straight and unyielding as a pine. The firelight played across his face, highlighting the sharp contours of his features.

In the glow of the flames, his eyes seemed softer than usual.

"Your Highness..." Su Nian stared at him, momentarily transfixed.

Xiao Yu steadied her and frowned. "Why did you come out? It's dangerous here."

"I wanted to help fight the fire..."

"Help with what?" Xiao Yu said, exasperated. "You can barely carry a bucket of water. You'd only get in the way."

Su Nian was stung into silence.

Xiao Yu sighed and pulled her back. "Come with me. It's not safe here."

He held her hand — not tightly, but with a grip that brooked no refusal.

Su Nian followed him, her heart beating faster with every step.

She instinctively tried to pull her hand away, but Xiao Yu's grip only tightened.

"Don't move."

Su Nian had no choice but to follow obediently.

They threaded through the chaotic crowd to a relatively safe area.

Xiao Yu released her hand and regarded her with a complex expression.

"You nearly fell just now. Do you realize what would have happened if I hadn't been there?"

Su Nian shook her head.

Xiao Yu stared at her for a long moment, then sighed. "Never mind. You're unharmed — that's what matters."

Su Nian saw the concern in his eyes, and a warm current surged through her.

"Your Highness, you are truly kind to me." The words slipped out before she could stop them.

Xiao Yu froze for an instant, then looked away. "I simply don't want the person I saved to come to harm, that is all."

With that, he turned and walked away, vanishing into the night.

Su Nian watched his retreating silhouette, and the corners of her mouth curved upward, unbidden.

This cold-faced prince — he was actually quite soft-hearted underneath it all.

That night, Su Nian spent the hours in Xiao Yu's study.

By the time the fire was brought under control, dawn was already breaking. Xiao Cui told her to rest for a while on the study's daybed. She was so exhausted that she fell asleep the moment she lay down.

When she woke the next morning, she found a thin blanket draped over her.

On the desk sat a steaming bowl of congee and several small dishes.

Beneath them lay a slip of paper, in Xiao Yu's handwriting:

"Eat your breakfast before you leave."

Su Nian stared at the note, her heart turning to mush.

This man really knew how to make a girl's heart flutter.

But then again — there could be nothing between them.

She was a transmigrator; sooner or later, she would return to the modern world. And she carried secrets that put her in constant danger.

She could not drag him into this.

Su Nian sighed and carefully folded the note, tucking it into her sleeve.

Then she picked up the bowl of congee and began to eat, one slow sip at a time.

Whatever happened, she would focus on living well in the present.

As for everything else — that could wait.

# Volume Two: The Pregnancy Storm


## Chapter 6: Discovery of Pregnancy

After that night, Su Nian continued her work in the study, copying ledgers.

Xiao Yu began visiting more frequently than before. Sometimes he would stay for an entire afternoon.

He still spoke little, but when she grew tired from writing, he would quietly have refreshments and tea sent in. When the weather turned, he would order the servants to bring her an extra layer of clothing.

Su Nian said nothing of it, but her heart was warm.

She knew it was wrong. Xiao Yu was a prince — he had a concubine, and in the future he would take a principal wife. She was merely a girl of unknown origins.

But she could not control her own heart.

Every time she saw him appear, her eyes would follow him, unbidden. Every time she heard his voice, her pulse would quicken without her consent.

She seemed to have... developed feelings for him.

Su Nian shook her head, trying to banish the thought.

No — she could not let this continue. She needed to find out the identity of those men in black as quickly as possible, and then leave the residence.

But some things were beyond her control.

Her body, for instance.

Lately, something had felt off.

She woke up each morning feeling nauseous. She had no appetite for anything. She tired more easily than usual and was always drowsy.

She assumed it was the summer heat — perhaps she had succumbed to heatstroke. She consulted a physician, who found nothing seriously wrong.

But Xiao Cui was not so sure. "Miss... could it be that you're... you know?"

Su Nian blinked. "Could be what?"

Xiao Cui's face turned red. "You know... *those* symptoms. When I served the lady of a household before, she was just like this when she was with child — always sick and always tired..."

Su Nian's heart lurched.

Pregnant? Surely not?

She had indeed missed her monthly courses for two months. But she had assumed it was her body still adjusting to the transmigration, or the stress causing her periods to stop.

But now that she thought about it...

Her face changed.

Two months ago — right after she had first transmigrated — she and Xiao Yu had shared a night together.

That evening, she had been running a high fever, delirious and confused. Xiao Yu had stayed by her bedside, tending to her without rest. In the middle of the night, half-conscious from the fever, she had mistaken him for her boyfriend, and one thing had led to another...

When she woke the next morning, she had wanted the earth to swallow her whole. Xiao Yu had looked equally uncomfortable and said he would take responsibility.

But afterward, an unspoken agreement had settled between them — neither ever mentioned it again, as though it had never happened.

Could it be... one time was all it took?

The more Su Nian thought about it, the more panicked she became. She hurriedly sent Xiao Cui to fetch a physician.

The physician arrived and carefully took her pulse.

After a moment, he rose, his expression a mixture of emotions.

"Young lady," he said, lowering his voice. "You are... with child."

Su Nian's mind went blank.

With child. Pregnant.

She was really pregnant.

With Xiao Yu's child.

"Sir..." she forced the words out, her voice barely audible. "Are you certain?"

The old physician stroked his beard. "I have practiced medicine for thirty years. I do not make mistakes. You are indeed carrying a child — approximately two months along."

Two months. The timeline matched.

Su Nian's mind was a whirlwind.

She had been in this world for barely two months, and now she was going to be a mother?

This was too fast.

"Young lady, what do you plan to do about the child?" the physician asked gently.

Su Nian pulled herself together and took a deep breath. "Doctor, can you keep this confidential? I don't want too many people to know just yet."

The physician hesitated. "Young lady, this is not something that can stay hidden forever. His Highness will find out sooner or later..."

"I know." Su Nian said. "But please, keep it quiet for now. Let me think it through first."

The physician nodded. "Very well. Be careful, young lady. The first three months are the most delicate. You should rest often and avoid overexertion."

"Thank you, Doctor."

Su Nian saw the physician out, then sat alone in her room, staring into space.

She was pregnant.

With Xiao Yu's child.

The news hit her like a bolt from a clear sky.

What was she supposed to do?

Tell Xiao Yu? What would he think? Would he want the child? Would he marry her?

Keep it hidden? But for how long? She would be found out eventually.

Or... terminate the pregnancy in secret?

The thought had barely surfaced before she rejected it outright.

No. This was a life. It was hers and Xiao Yu's child. She had no right to take its life.

But what about after it was born? An unmarried woman bearing a child of unknown paternity — in this world, she would be the target of endless scorn.

Even if Xiao Yu was willing to take responsibility, would Consort De consent? Would Concubine Liu let the matter rest?

And what about the men in black who had pursued her? What would they do when they learned she was carrying a child?

A cascade of questions swirled in her mind.

Su Nian pressed her fingers to her temples, feeling as though her head might split open.

She needed to stay calm. She needed to think carefully about what to do next.

## Chapter 7: Prince's Wrath

Before Su Nian could figure out what to do, trouble came looking for her.

That afternoon, she was sitting in the courtyard, basking in the sun, when Xiao Yu appeared without warning.

His face was dark as a thundercloud — the look of a sky before a violent storm.

Su Nian's heart skipped a beat. "Your Highness? What is it?"

Xiao Yu said nothing. He simply waved his hand, dismissing the servants.

Xiao Cui hesitated, then tactfully withdrew.

They were alone in the courtyard.

Xiao Yu stared at Su Nian for a long moment before speaking. "I am going to ask you one question. Answer me truthfully."

Su Nian felt a prickle of unease. "Please go ahead, Your Highness."

"You have been unwell lately. What is the matter?"

Su Nian's heart tightened. How did he know?

"It is nothing serious — just some mild discomfort."

Xiao Yu stepped closer. "You saw a physician? What did the physician say?"

Su Nian took a step back, uncertain whether she should tell the truth.

When she did not answer, a complex emotion flickered in Xiao Yu's eyes. "I have already spoken with the physician."

Su Nian's face drained of color.

Xiao Yu drew a slow breath. His voice was ice. "You are pregnant."

It was not a question. It was a statement.

Su Nian knew she could hide it no longer. She nodded. "Yes."

The words had barely left her lips when a sharp crack rang out — his palm connected with her cheek.

The force of the blow sent her staggering. She nearly fell.

Su Nian clutched her face and stared at Xiao Yu in disbelief.

He had struck her?

Xiao Yu's hand still hung in the air. He seemed momentarily surprised by his own action, but his expression quickly hardened back into cold indifference.

"Tell me — whose child is this?"

Su Nian was stunned.

He was asking whose child it was?

Did he really think...

"Your Highness," she forced the words out, her voice breaking. "The child is yours."

Xiao Yu gave a cold laugh. "Mine? You presume rather a lot. I am the Prince Yu — how could I ever have been involved with a woman of unknown origins like you?"

Su Nian felt as though a blade had been driven through her heart.

"You think I don't know?" Xiao Yu's voice was tight with suppressed fury. "That night, when I was caring for you, you took advantage and threw yourself at me! I thought you were a respectable woman. I had no idea you were so shameless!"

Su Nian's face went deathly pale.

That night... she had been delirious with fever, and Xiao Yu had been a willing participant. How could he say such things?

"Your Highness, you misunderstand. That night, I was —"

"Enough!" Xiao Yu cut her off. "Did you think I would believe your lies? You merely want to use this child to elevate your position! But you have miscalculated!"

Su Nian looked at him. Her eyes grew red.

Was this the man she had fallen for?

She had believed that beneath his cold exterior, he was a good person. She had believed that though he spoke little, he cared for her in his heart.

And the truth?

He did not believe a single word she said. From beginning to end, he had regarded her as nothing more than a scheming woman trying to attach herself to a powerful man.

"You think I don't know?" Xiao Yu sneered. "Your body has issues — your monthly courses are irregular, and you are not easily able to conceive. You used this opportunity to entrap me and marry into the residence, didn't you?"

Su Nian froze.

Her body had issues? This body... was not easily able to conceive?

No — she did not know the original owner's medical history. In her own body, her periods had always been perfectly regular.

"The physician has examined you," Xiao Yu's voice was glacial. "You have a congenital cold constitution, which makes conception difficult. This pregnancy — you must have used some folk remedy to bring it about. Am I right?"

Su Nian opened her mouth to speak, but found she had no defense.

She had only been in this body for two months. Her understanding of its medical history was limited. She did not even know whether the original owner had any underlying conditions.

But how could Xiao Yu think this of her?

He was her savior, and she did have feelings for him. But she had never once entertained the thought of using a child to climb the social ladder!

"Your Highness," her voice was hoarse. "The child is truly yours. If you don't believe me, we can perform a blood test."

"A blood test?" Xiao Yu laughed coldly. "You think I would accept a child of unknown parentage?"

Su Nian's heart went completely cold.

Did that mean... he did not want this child?

"I will give you a sum of silver. Leave the residence, and from this day forward, we will have nothing more to do with each other."

Xiao Yu's words were like a bucket of icy water, drenching her to the bone.

Su Nian stared at him, and the tears she had been holding back finally spilled over.

"Your Highness, how can you say that?" Her voice cracked with sobs. "The child is innocent! Even if you don't want me, you cannot abandon the child!"

A complex emotion flashed through Xiao Yu's eyes, but it was swiftly extinguished by coldness.

"My bloodline will not be left to wander the world," he said. "I will keep this child. But you — you must leave."

Su Nian was stunned.

Keep the child, but send the mother away?

What kind of logic was that?

She was the child's mother. How could he tear them apart?

"No," Su Nian shook her head. "I won't go. I will raise this child myself."

Xiao Yu frowned. "You are a lone woman with nothing. How will you raise a child?"

"I will find a way." Su Nian wiped her tears. "No matter how hard it gets, I will never abandon my child."

Xiao Yu stared at her for a long time. A faint, almost imperceptible emotion stirred in his eyes.

At last, he turned away.

At the doorway, he paused. "In three days, you will leave the residence. I will arrange for someone to escort you."

With that, he strode away without looking back.

Su Nian watched his retreating figure, tears streaming down her face.

This was the man she had fallen in love with.

Cold-blooded. Selfish. He did not care about her feelings at all.

She had been blind to have ever loved him.

But the child...

She pressed a hand to her belly, and the tears fell harder.

I'm sorry, little one. Your mother has failed you.

Your father does not want you. But your mother will love you with all her heart.

## Chapter 8: Difficult Choices

That night, Su Nian cried alone under her blanket until dawn.

She thought about many things.

Xiao Yu did not want her or the child. She had to leave.

But where could she go?

She was a transmigrator who knew nothing of this world. She had no identification, no money, no connections.

In this feudal society, an unmarried woman with a child was virtually doomed.

Could she... really only terminate the pregnancy?

No! Absolutely not!

This was hers and Xiao Yu's child. The only family she had in this lifetime.

She could not lose it.

But how would she survive with a child?

"Miss, you haven't eaten all day. Xiao Cui made you some chicken soup — please, have at least a little..."

Mid-sentence, she caught sight of Su Nian's red, swollen eyes and panicked. "Miss, why are you crying? What happened?"

Su Nian shook her head. She did not want to talk.

Xiao Cui set the soup on the table and stamped her foot in anxiety. "Miss, tell Xiao Cui! Has someone been bullying you?"

Xiao Cui froze. "Leave? Go where?"

Su Nian gave a bitter smile. "His Highness has ordered me to leave the residence."

"Why?" Xiao Cui was distraught. "Wasn't His Highness treating you well? Why would he suddenly ask you to go?"

Su Nian shook her head. She did not tell Xiao Cui about the pregnancy.

This was her burden alone. She would not drag Xiao Cui into it.

"Xiao Cui," she said, "after I leave, you should stay at the residence. His Highness will not mistreat you."

Xiao Cui's tears spilled over at once. "Miss, Xiao Cui won't stay! Xiao Cui wants to follow you! Wherever you go, Xiao Cui goes!"

Even in this world, there was someone who genuinely cared for her.

"Xiao Cui, following me will mean hardship."

"Xiao Cui isn't afraid!" Xiao Cui wiped her tears. "Xiao Cui was beaten and chased by slave traders before — she's endured far worse. As long as she can stay by Miss's side, Xiao Cui can bear anything!"

Su Nian was silent for a moment, then nodded. "All right. Then we leave together."

Mistress and maid embraced, weeping together.

Su Nian's expression darkened. "Your Ladyship should choose your words carefully. There is nothing between me and His Highness."

"Nothing?" Liu Ru Yan scoffed. "Nothing, and yet he lets you live in the residence? Nothing, and yet he treats you so well? I'd say you used some seductive trick to lure him in!"

Su Nian took a deep breath, suppressing her anger. "I've told you — my relationship with His Highness is entirely proper. If Your Ladyship has nothing else to say, I will take my leave."

"Stand where you are!" Liu Ru Yan's face contorted with rage. "You lowly wretch — how dare you talk back to me?"

Liu Ru Yan had not expected resistance. She stumbled and fell to the ground.

"How dare you! You struck me!" Liu Ru Yan screamed. "Someone! Seize this harlot!"

Several maids immediately surrounded Su Nian.

Su Nian retreated a few steps, a bad feeling rising in her chest.

She was pregnant — she could not afford to fight. But if she did not defend herself, Liu Ru Yan would walk all over her.

At that critical moment, an icy voice rang out:

"Stop."

Everyone turned. Xiao Yu had appeared at the courtyard gate at some unknown moment, watching them with frosty disapproval.

"Your Highness!" Liu Ru Yan instantly adopted a wronged expression. "Your Highness, you must stand up for me! This wretch attacked me — she pushed me to the ground!"

Xiao Yu did not look at her. His gaze settled on Su Nian.

Her clothes were disheveled, and tear tracks stained her cheeks. She looked utterly wretched.

Something flickered in his eyes, but his expression quickly returned to its usual coldness. "What happened?"

Liu Ru Yan spoke first. "Your Highness, I was only asking where she was going, and she attacked me without provocation..."

"Be quiet." Xiao Yu's voice was glacial.

The words died in Liu Ru Yan's throat. She dared not speak again.

Xiao Yu looked at Su Nian. "Where were you going?"

Su Nian lowered her eyes. "I was going to sell some jewelry... to get some silver."

Xiao Yu's brow creased. "What do you need silver for?"

"Go!"

Only Xiao Yu and Su Nian remained in the courtyard.

The silence was thick with awkwardness.

Xiao Yu looked at her, seeming to want to say something but thinking better of it.

In the end, all he said was, "Go back and rest. We will speak tomorrow."

He turned and walked away.

Su Nian watched him go, her emotions a tangled knot.

Why had he intervened just now? Was it because he did not want anyone mistreated on his grounds? Or was it because... he still had feelings for her?

Whatever the case, she was leaving.

This child — she would protect it, no matter what.

## Chapter 9: Rumors in the Residence

News of Su Nian's pregnancy somehow leaked.

The entire residence was abuzz with gossip.

"Did you hear? That Miss Su is carrying the prince's child!"

"Is it true? But she's a woman of unknown origins!"

"Who knows? She probably used some underhanded trick to seduce the prince."

"Tsk — the nerve of her. A lone orphan, daring to throw herself at the prince?"

"I say the child isn't even his. Why would the prince ever look twice at her?"

The rumors were like knives, each one piercing Su Nian's heart.

She knew how harsh this society was toward women. To be pregnant out of wedlock was scandal enough; add her unknown origins, and she became an easy target for everyone's contempt.

But what could she do?

She was not the original owner of this body. Her thinking came from the modern world. She did not believe she had done anything wrong.

But in this world, it was an unforgivable sin.

Su Nian shut herself in her room, afraid to go out.

She dreaded the pointing fingers and whispered insults. Even more, she dreaded running into Xiao Yu.

Since that day, Xiao Yu had not come to see her.

She did not know what he was thinking. She did not know where he truly stood regarding the child.

Never mind. She did not want to think about it.

She had already made her decision to leave. In three days, once she was ready, she would take Xiao Cui and flee the Capital City — go somewhere no one knew her, give birth to the child, and raise it in peace.

As for Xiao Yu...

They were destined to be together yet never to be.

That afternoon, Su Nian was sitting in her room, lost in thought, when Xiao Cui came rushing in.

"Miss! Miss! Something terrible has happened!"

Su Nian frowned. "What is it?"

Xiao Cui was breathless. "The rumors outside — they're saying... saying the child in your belly isn't the prince's! They're saying you've been carrying on with the young master of the Gu family — that the child is Gu Yan's!"

Su Nian was stunned.

Gu Yan? She didn't even know anyone named Gu Yan!

"Who is spreading this?" she demanded.

"I don't know!" Xiao Cui was in tears. "But everyone is talking about it! They're saying the most awful things — that you're a loose woman, that seducing the prince wasn't enough for you, so you went after the Gu family's son as well..."

Su Nian's expression hardened.

Someone was framing her.

She had never even heard the name Gu Yan. This was clearly a deliberate fabrication designed to destroy her reputation.

Was it Liu Ru Yan? Or someone else?

She took a deep breath and forced herself to stay calm.

This was not the time to pursue the truth. She needed to find a way to clear her name first.

"Xiao Cui, do you know who Gu Yan is?"

"I do!" Xiao Cui nodded. "Gu Yan is the son of Lord Gu, the Chief Physician of the Imperial Medical Court. People call him the Jade Gentleman — he's quite famous in the Capital City. But Miss, you don't even know him! How could you possibly be involved with him?"

Su Nian gave a bitter smile. "I don't know him either. These rumors were definitely planted by someone with malicious intent."

"Then what do we do?" Xiao Cui was frantic. "If this keeps spreading, your reputation will be ruined!"

Su Nian was silent for a moment. "Come. We're going to find His Highness."

She could not hide any longer.

If she kept hiding, the rumors would be treated as truth.

She needed to go to Xiao Yu herself and explain. Even if he did not believe her, she had to try.

Su Nian and Xiao Cui set off to find Xiao Yu.

But he was not in the study, nor in the main courtyard. They asked several servants, but none knew where the prince had gone.

Just as her anxiety was reaching its peak, a gentle voice spoke from behind her.

"Are you looking for Prince Yu?"

Su Nian turned. A young man in white stood a short distance away.

He was in his early twenties, with a refined, jade-like beauty and an otherworldly air. He wore a white brocade robe, a jade belt at his waist, and carried a folding fan — the very image of a gentleman stepped out of a painting.

Su Nian blinked. "And you are?"

"I am Gu Yan." The man smiled faintly. "I have the pleasure of making your acquaintance, Miss Su."

Su Nian's heart lurched.

Gu Yan?!

The very Gu Yan she was rumored to be involved with?!

What an incredible coincidence — meeting him here, of all places?

Gu Yan seemed to sense her surprise and smiled. "Please don't be alarmed, Miss. I heard that someone has been spreading rumors about the two of us, so I came specifically to clear things up with you."

Su Nian breathed a sigh of relief. "So you've heard the rumors too, Lord Gu."

Gu Yan nodded. "The rumors are clearly the work of someone with a deliberate agenda. You and I have never met before — how could there possibly be any impropriety between us?"

"You speak the truth, Lord Gu." Su Nian said. "I just don't know who would go to such lengths to slander me."

Gu Yan considered for a moment. "I have my suspicions. But without evidence, I cannot say for certain."

"Please, go ahead."

"Consider this: who benefits most from these rumors?" Gu Yan asked.

Su Nian thought for a moment. "Whoever hates me most would be the most likely to spread such lies."

Gu Yan nodded. "Exactly. Who have you offended?"

Su Nian thought of Liu Ru Yan.

That night, Liu Ru Yan had been pushed to the ground by her — she must have harbored a grudge. And Liu Ru Yan had been trying to frame Su Nian and drive her out of the residence from the start.

Could it be her?

But Liu Ru Yan alone could not have spread rumors on such a scale. She must have backing from someone more powerful.

"Thank you for the warning, Lord Gu." Su Nian curtsied. "I will be careful."

Gu Yan smiled gently. "You are too polite, Miss. Actually, these rumors have caused great damage to your reputation. As one of the parties involved, it is only right that I help you set the record straight."

"I am grateful for your willingness to speak up for me, Lord Gu."

Gu Yan looked at her, and an unusual gleam flickered in his eyes. "Think nothing of it. I have other matters to attend to, so I will take my leave. If there is anything you need, do not hesitate to seek me out."

With that, he turned and walked away.

Su Nian watched him go, a nagging feeling tugging at her.

This Gu Yan... he seemed rather too eager to help.

Never mind — this was not the time to dwell on such things. She still needed to find Xiao Yu and explain everything.

## Chapter 10: Unexpected Fainting

Su Nian searched for a while before finally finding Xiao Yu in the garden pavilion.

Su Nian took a deep breath and walked over.

"Your Highness."

"Why are you here?"

Su Nian sat down across from him. "Your Highness, I have something I wish to say to you."

Xiao Yu set down his teacup. "Speak."

"The rumors outside — you've heard them too, haven't you?" Su Nian looked him straight in the eye. "That child is not Gu Yan's. It is yours. I don't even know Gu Yan."

Consort De. Xiao Yu's own mother.

So it was her all along.

"Why would she do such a thing?" Su Nian asked.

Xiao Yu fell silent for a moment. "Because I refused to marry the woman she chose for me. She wanted to use this opportunity to drive you out of the residence."

Su Nian gave a bitter smile.

So that was it.

This was the game of power. She was nothing more than a pawn — used and framed by others.

"What does Your Highness plan to do?" she asked.

Xiao Yu looked at her. "What do you want me to do?"

Su Nian froze.

He was asking her?

Wasn't this supposed to be his decision?

"Your Highness," she said carefully, "whatever you decide, I will respect your choice. But this child is innocent. I hope you will spare his life."

A complex emotion flickered in Xiao Yu's eyes.

After a long while, he spoke. "I said before — I will keep this child."

Su Nian's heart leaped with joy. "Does Your Highness mean..."

Xiao Yu cut her off. "But you — must leave."

Su Nian's heart went cold again.

Those words again.

He would keep the child, but he did not want her.

What was she to this man? A tool for bearing children?

"Your Highness," she could not help asking, "do you truly have no feelings for me whatsoever?"

Xiao Yu was taken aback.

He looked at her — at her red, swollen eyes, at her stubborn expression — and felt as though something had pierced his heart.

That night, he had lost control.

He had thought he could restrain himself, but when he saw her burning with fever, deliriously calling his name, his heart had been thrown into chaos.

It was not that he had no feelings for her.

He simply dared not admit it.

He was Prince Yu — the prince the Emperor feared most. His every move was watched. If he developed feelings for a woman of unknown origins, it would become a weapon against him.

And so he had always suppressed his emotions.

But now...

"Su Nian," he said, his voice somewhat hoarse, "do you have any idea how much trouble you have brought me?"

Su Nian lowered her gaze. "I know."

"Because I saved you, I was reprimanded by my mother. Because you live in my residence, I have been impeached by the censors. Because of your pregnancy, I have been dragged into endless gossip."

Xiao Yu spoke each word deliberately, and with every sentence, Su Nian's heart grew colder.

She knew. She knew she had brought him a great deal of trouble.

But she had never wanted any of this.

She just wanted to live her life in peace. But fate would not allow it.

"Therefore," Xiao Yu's voice turned cold, "I must let you go. Only when you leave will these troubles disappear."

Tears welled up in Su Nian's eyes again.

She wanted to argue, to tell him she did not care about the trouble. But the words died on her lips.

He was right.

As long as she remained at the residence for even one more day, he would be plagued by these matters. He was a prince — bound by duties he could not escape.

"I understand." Su Nian wiped her tears and stood up. "In three days, I will leave."

Xiao Yu looked at her without speaking.

Su Nian turned to leave, but suddenly the world began to spin around her.

Her body swayed, and she fell backward —

"Su Nian!" Xiao Yu reacted with lightning speed, catching her in his arms.

Su Nian leaned against his chest, her face as pale as paper.

"I will send for a physician!" Xiao Yu was frantic. He scooped her up and ran toward the house.

In her daze, Su Nian could hear his heartbeat — rapid and powerful.

Was he worried about her?

She tried to speak, but no words would come.

Her eyelids grew heavier and heavier, until she lost consciousness entirely.

# Volume Three: Growing Affection


## Chapter 11: Tender Care

When Su Nian awoke, she found herself lying on an unfamiliar bed.

Calming incense burned in the room, and the faint scent of medicine lingered in the air.

She turned her head and saw Xiao Yu sitting by the bedside, holding a bowl of medicine.

His expression was grim, and there was an unmistakable weariness and worry between his brows.

"Your Highness?" Su Nian stared at him in confusion. "Why are you here?"

Xiao Yu did not answer. He simply brought the medicine bowl to her lips. "Drink."

Su Nian opened her mouth to say something, but Xiao Yu tipped the bowl without giving her a chance to refuse.

Bitter! So bitter she couldn't help but grimace!

But she gritted her teeth and drank it all.

Xiao Yu set down the bowl, his voice low. "The physician said your body is weak and you have been overwrought with worry. You nearly miscarried."

Su Nian's face changed.

Nearly miscarried?

Her baby... nearly lost?

"Fortunately, you were treated in time," Xiao Yu continued. "The child is safe. But you must stay in bed and rest. You are not to wander about."

Su Nian looked at him, unsure of what to say.

Hadn't he just said he wanted her to leave? So why now...

"Your Highness," she ventured carefully, "didn't you say I should leave in three days?"

Xiao Yu fell silent.

After a long while, he spoke. "In your current condition, you cannot leave."

Su Nian gave a bitter smile.

Of course.

He was not keeping her because he was worried about her — only because of the child in her womb.

"Please do not worry, Your Highness," she said. "Once I am a little better, I will leave immediately. I will not cause you any more trouble."

A barely perceptible emotion flickered in Xiao Yu's eyes.

"Do you want to leave that badly?"

Su Nian was taken aback.

Wasn't he the one who told her to go? Why was he asking her?

"Your Highness," she said carefully, "I do not wish to be a burden to you. If you want me to go, I will go."

Xiao Yu's gaze darkened.

He stood up abruptly and walked to the window, turning his back to her.

"Su Nian," his voice was low, "have you ever thought about what will happen to you if you leave?"

Su Nian fell silent.

She had thought about it.

An unmarried woman, carrying a child — in this ancient world, she would have no way to survive.

She had no identification, no money, no connections. Even if she fled to the ends of the earth, she would be found.

Not to mention, she was pregnant and her body was so weak.

She could not leave.

But she could not stay either.

Staying at the residence made her his greatest liability. Consort De, Concubine Liu, the censors — everyone wanted her gone.

"I will take things one step at a time," she said softly. "There will always be a way."

Xiao Yu turned to look at her.

His gaze was bottomless — she could not fathom what he was thinking.

"Do you really have so little faith in me?" he asked.

Su Nian froze. "What do you mean?"

Xiao Yu did not answer. He walked back to the bed and sat down beside her.

He reached out and gently stroked her cheek.

The gesture was so tender it did not seem like him at all.

Su Nian's heart skipped a beat.

"Your Highness..."

"Don't move." Xiao Yu's voice was low. "Let me look at your injury."

Su Nian then remembered — her face had been slapped by Xiao Yu before.

Though several days had passed, there should still be bruising.

Xiao Yu's fingers lightly brushed her cheek, and a flicker of self-reproach crossed his eyes.

"Does it hurt?"

Su Nian shook her head.

It actually hurt a little. But she did not want him to feel guilty.

Xiao Yu sighed and withdrew his hand.

"I'm sorry."

Su Nian froze.

He was apologizing to her?

Xiao Yu — the cold-hearted Prince Yu — was actually apologizing to her?

"I should not have struck you that day," Xiao Yu said. "I was just... too angry."

Su Nian looked at him, unsure of what to say.

Xiao Yu continued, "When the news of your pregnancy reached me, I was truly shocked. I thought you were trying to use the child to elevate your status, so I said many hurtful things."

Tears welled up in Su Nian's eyes again.

She did not want to cry. But she could not help it.

She had endured so many grievances these past days.

Bullied by Liu Ru Yan, framed by Consort De, gossiped about by everyone in the residence...

She had been holding it in, gritting her teeth and pressing on.

But now, a single "I'm sorry" from Xiao Yu caused all her fortitude to crumble.

"Don't cry." Xiao Yu looked at her, at a loss. "I'm hopeless when women cry."

Su Nian laughed through her tears. "Your Highness is so formidable — how could you be afraid of a woman's tears?"

Xiao Yu paused, and the corners of his mouth curved slightly.

It was the first time he had smiled in a long while.

Su Nian looked at his smile, and her heart skipped another beat.

This man truly was handsome when he smiled.

"Rest well," Xiao Yu stood up. "I will come see you tomorrow."

Su Nian nodded. "Take care, Your Highness."

Xiao Yu reached the doorway and suddenly stopped.

"Su Nian."

"Is there anything else, Your Highness?"

Xiao Yu did not turn around. "If anything troubles you in the future, tell me directly. Do not bear it all alone."

Su Nian paused, her eyes reddening again.

"Thank you, Your Highness."

Xiao Yu said no more and strode away.

Su Nian watched his retreating figure, a warmth flooding her heart.

Perhaps... things were not so bad after all.

Perhaps, he did have her in his heart.

## Chapter 12: Frank Communication

In the days that followed, Xiao Yu came to visit Su Nian every day.

Sometimes in the morning, sometimes in the evening, and sometimes just a fleeting glance before he left.

He did not say much, but each time he came, he brought something she liked to eat.

Sometimes pastries, sometimes fruit, sometimes bird's nest bestowed by the palace.

Su Nian said nothing, but her heart was warm.

Xiao Cui watched from the side and could not resist teasing her. "Miss, His Highness is truly kind to you."

Su Nian shot her a disapproving look. "Don't talk nonsense. His Highness is only concerned about the child."

"That's not true at all." Xiao Cui covered her mouth, giggling. "The way His Highness looks at you is different now. I think he must have feelings for you."

Su Nian's face flushed.

She dared not admit it, but she had felt the same way.

Xiao Yu's attitude toward her had noticeably softened. Sometimes he would even chat with her and ask about the modern world.

She had not hidden the fact that she was a transmigrator — she had told him everything in full detail.

Xiao Yu listened in silence for a long time.

"You're saying you come from a thousand years in the future?"

Su Nian nodded. "I know it sounds incredible. But it is the truth."

Xiao Yu looked at her. "Then why did you appear here?"

Su Nian shook her head. "I don't know either. That night I was working late, and suddenly everything went dark. When I woke up, I was in this world."

Xiao Yu frowned. "What is the world like over there?"

Su Nian thought for a moment and began telling him about modern life.

She spoke of cars, airplanes, television, mobile phones... of skyscrapers, the internet, artificial intelligence...

Xiao Yu listened in stunned amazement.

"The world a thousand years from now is truly that miraculous?"

Su Nian nodded. "Yes. Technology has advanced so rapidly. There are things you could not even imagine."

Xiao Yu looked at her, a complex emotion flickering in his eyes.

"Then... do you want to go back?"

Su Nian fell silent for a moment.

Go back? Of course she wanted to.

She wanted to return to the modern world, to resume her life, to see her friends and colleagues, to order takeout, drink bubble tea, scroll through her phone...

But now...

"I don't know," she said honestly. "I originally wanted to go back. But now..."

She placed a hand on her belly.

Now she was carrying his child. How could she leave and go back alone?

Xiao Yu understood her meaning.

He did not speak. He simply looked at her in silence.

That gaze was deep and complex — she could not read his thoughts.

After a long while, he spoke. "If one day you could go back — would you?"

Su Nian looked at him and thought carefully.

"I don't know," she said. "Perhaps I would. Perhaps I wouldn't."

Xiao Yu frowned. "Why?"

Su Nian smiled. "Because there are people here I care about."

Xiao Yu's gaze flickered.

He looked at her, and she looked at him.

Their eyes met, and the air seemed to still.

Su Nian's face grew redder and redder, her heartbeat faster and faster.

Had she just said too much?

How could she say such a thing in front of Xiao Yu?

Just as she was about to explain, Xiao Yu suddenly spoke:

"The person you care about — is it me?"

Su Nian froze.

How could he ask so directly?

She wanted to deny it, but the words would not come.

Because... yes.

The person she cared about was him.

From that night onward, her heart had already belonged to him.

Xiao Yu looked at her, waiting for her answer.

Su Nian took a deep breath and finally nodded.

"Yes," she said. "The person I care about is you."

A glimmer of joy flashed in Xiao Yu's eyes.

He stepped forward and took her hand.

"Su Nian, I also..."

Before he could finish, a commotion erupted outside the door.

"Your Highness! Your Highness!" The guard's voice was urgent. "Someone has come from the palace! His Majesty summons you to the palace immediately!"

Xiao Yu's expression darkened.

He released Su Nian's hand. "I must go deal with this. Rest well."

Su Nian nodded. "Be careful, Your Highness."

Xiao Yu gave her one last look, then turned and left.

Su Nian watched his retreating figure, her heart caught between sweetness and worry.

What had he been about to say? Was he going to say... that he cared about her too?

She could not be certain.

But she knew that the relationship between them had changed.

## Chapter 13: Deepening Feelings

After Xiao Yu entered the palace, he did not return for a very long time.

Su Nian lay in bed, tossing and turning, unable to sleep.

She was thinking about Xiao Yu. And about the words he had left unfinished.

He had said, "I also..."

Also what?

Also cared about her?

Or was it merely her imagination?

Su Nian sighed and forced her eyes shut.

Never mind. She should stop thinking about it.

Even if he had feelings for her, they could never truly be together.

She was a transmigrator with a mysterious identity. He was a prince of the realm, bound by duty.

Even if they loved each other, they could not escape the cruelty of reality.

Just then, a knock came at the door.

"Miss, are you asleep?" It was Xiao Cui's voice.

Su Nian sat up. "Come in."

Xiao Cui pushed the door open, her face grave. "Miss, something has happened."

Su Nian's heart tightened. "What is it?"

"His Highness has been detained in the palace!" Xiao Cui said urgently. "I heard that someone has accused him of forming factions and plotting rebellion!"

Su Nian's face went pale.

Rebellion?!

That was a crime punishable by the extermination of nine generations of one's clan!

"How could this happen?" she asked.

"I don't know!" Xiao Cui wiped her tears. "But everyone outside is saying that His Highness is in grave danger this time!"

Su Nian's heart felt as though it were being squeezed.

Ignoring her weakened body, she struggled out of bed.

"I must go to the palace!"

"Miss, you can't!" Xiao Cui blocked her. "You're pregnant — you must not exert yourself!"

"Let me go!" Su Nian was frantic. "Xiao Yu is in trouble — I cannot just stand by and do nothing!"

"Miss, please calm down!" Xiao Cui cried. "What could you possibly accomplish by going now? You would only make things worse! You might even implicate His Highness!"

Su Nian froze.

Xiao Cui was right.

What could she possibly do right now?

She did not even understand the basic rules of this world. Rushing into the palace would only cause more trouble for Xiao Yu.

But she could not simply do nothing, either.

"What should I do..." she murmured to herself. "How can I save him..."

Just then, footsteps sounded outside the door.

"Miss Su."

Su Nian looked up to see Gu Yan standing at the entrance.

He was still dressed in his white silk robes, looking as refined and gentle as ever.

"Master Gu?" Su Nian was surprised. "Why are you here?"

Gu Yan stepped inside and gave a slight bow. "I heard that Prince Yu is in trouble, so I came to see how you are faring."

Su Nian gave a bitter smile. "I am but a weak woman — what could I possibly do?"

Gu Yan looked at her, a complex emotion in his eyes.

"Miss, I do have an idea."

Su Nian's eyes lit up. "What is it?"

Gu Yan pondered for a moment. "His Majesty is detaining Prince Yu because someone has accused him of rebellion. If we can find evidence proving that those accusations are false, His Majesty should release him."

Su Nian nodded. "That makes sense, but how do we prove they are false?"

Gu Yan smiled faintly. "I know someone who can help."

"Who?"

"Senior Matron Cui, who serves Consort De."

Su Nian's heart stirred.

Senior Matron Cui? The same Senior Matron Cui who had helped spread the rumors?

"Why would she help us?"

Gu Yan lowered his voice. "Although Senior Matron Cui serves Consort De, she has her own interests. If we offer her sufficient incentive, she should be willing to tell the truth."

Su Nian frowned. "What truth?"

Gu Yan glanced around to make sure no outsiders were present before speaking:

"In truth, all those memorials accusing Prince Yu of rebellion were orchestrated by Consort De. She did this to force Prince Yu into submission — to make him marry the woman she has chosen as his principal wife."

Su Nian gasped.

Consort De... to force Xiao Yu into submission, she had actually framed him for rebellion?

That was truly vicious!

"Isn't she afraid of being exposed?" Su Nian asked.

Gu Yan shook his head. "She believed it would not be uncovered. But she forgot that human hearts are greedy. Senior Matron Cui holds evidence. If we can obtain it, we can clear Prince Yu's name."

Su Nian fell silent.

It was indeed a plan.

But...

She looked at Gu Yan, feeling that something was not quite right.

Why was Gu Yan helping her?

She barely knew him — why would he take such a great risk on her behalf?

"Master Gu," she said, "why are you helping me?"

Gu Yan paused, then smiled. "I am helping you because I have my own motives, of course."

Su Nian's heart tightened. "What motives?"

Gu Yan looked at her, an unusual gleam in his eyes.

"Miss, there are some things I cannot say right now. But once Prince Yu's situation is resolved, I will tell you."

Su Nian frowned.

She did not like being kept in the dark.

But with Xiao Yu in danger, she could not afford to be picky.

"Master Gu, I am in your debt, then."

Gu Yan nodded. "Rest assured, Miss. I will go to Senior Matron Cui now. By tomorrow at the latest, I should have news."

With that, he turned and left.

Su Nian watched his retreating figure, a faint unease stirring in her heart.

Just what was Gu Yan really after?

## Chapter 14: Palace Banquet Turmoil

After Gu Yan left, Su Nian did not sleep at all that night.

She worried about Xiao Yu. She also wondered about Gu Yan's true intentions.

The next day at noon, Gu Yan brought news as promised.

"Miss, it is done!" he said with a smile. "Senior Matron Cui has agreed to hand over the evidence!"

Su Nian breathed a sigh of relief. "Wonderful! Then we must hurry to the palace and present the evidence to His Majesty!"

Gu Yan nodded. "I have already made the arrangements. There is a banquet at the palace tonight, and His Majesty will host all the civil and military officials. I will take you into the palace then."

Su Nian hesitated. "Can a woman like me even enter the palace?"

"You are Prince Yu's person — of course you can enter the palace," Gu Yan said. "If we can save Prince Yu, reputations hardly matter."

Su Nian thought for a moment, then nodded in agreement.

For Xiao Yu, she was willing to do anything.

That evening, Su Nian changed into the clothes Gu Yan had prepared and followed him into the palace.

This was her first time entering the palace since transmigrating.

The palace was vast and magnificent, but she had no heart to admire it. She only wanted to see Xiao Yu as quickly as possible.

The banquet was held in the Great Ming Hall.

Su Nian followed Gu Yan inside. The hall was ablaze with lights, filled with music and dancing.

Civil and military officials gathered in full force, toasting and drinking in a lively atmosphere.

She scanned the crowd and finally spotted Xiao Yu.

He sat in the seat below the Emperor, his face somewhat pale, looking exhausted.

But his back was still ramrod straight, his gaze still cold and sharp.

Su Nian's heart ached.

He must have suffered greatly these past few days.

Just then, Xiao Yu seemed to sense something and turned to look in her direction.

Their eyes met across the room.

A flash of surprise crossed Xiao Yu's eyes, followed by worry and anger.

He stood up, about to come toward her, but was blocked by the guards beside him.

Su Nian saw his lips move — he seemed to be saying something.

Though they were too far apart for her to hear, she could guess.

He must be saying: "Why are you here? Leave at once!"

Su Nian shook her head, signaling him not to worry.

She turned to Gu Yan. "Where is the evidence?"

Gu Yan smiled faintly and produced a stack of papers from his sleeve.

"This is the evidence Senior Matron Cui gave me. With this, Prince Yu can clear his name."

Su Nian nodded and was about to present it to the Emperor when she suddenly sensed something was wrong.

She looked at Gu Yan and noticed that he was staring at Xiao Yu, a cold gleam in his eyes.

"Master Gu," she frowned, "what exactly are you trying to do?"

Gu Yan's smile deepened. "Miss, did you think I was helping you for Prince Yu's sake?"

Su Nian's heart clenched. "What do you mean?"

Gu Yan did not answer. He turned to face Xiao Yu and said in a loud voice:

"His Highness Prince Yu, someone asked me to deliver a message to you."

Xiao Yu's cold gaze fell upon him.

Gu Yan continued, "She says: your child is in my hands. If you want the child to live, confess your crimes."

Su Nian's face went deathly pale.

She looked down at her belly, a surge of fear rising within her.

How could she have been so foolish?

Gu Yan had clearly come prepared — how could he genuinely want to help her?

"Gu Yan," she took a step back, "who are you really?"

Gu Yan looked at her. The smile vanished from his face, replaced by an icy mask.

"Who am I?" he sneered. "I am Prince Yelü Qi of the Northern Di."

Su Nian's heart plummeted.

A prince of Northern Di?!

She remembered now. Xiao Yu was the God of War — his name struck terror into the hearts of the Northern Di people. It was only natural that they would want him eliminated.

So Gu Yan — no, Yelü Qi — had been targeting Xiao Yu from the very start!

"The rumors — you spread them?" Su Nian asked.

Yelü Qi nodded. "Indeed. I arranged for people to spread the rumors, to sow discord between Consort De and Prince Yu. I thought I could use Consort De's hand to destroy him. But Prince Yu's luck held — he survived."

Su Nian's face was ashen. "So you changed your approach?"

Yelü Qi sneered. "Indeed. I deliberately approached you and gained your trust. Then I seized the opportunity to bring you into the palace and use you as leverage against Prince Yu."

Su Nian finally understood.

She had thought she had found a benefactor, but she had invited a wolf into her home.

In this world, no one helped anyone without reason.

She had been too naive.

"You want to use me to threaten Xiao Yu?" Su Nian asked. "But what good does that do you?"

Yelü Qi smiled. "If Prince Yu confesses, my Northern Di can win without fighting. Prince Yu is the Great Zhou Dynasty's greatest shield. Without him, Great Zhou is ours for the taking."

Su Nian's heart sank to the very bottom.

She looked at Xiao Yu. His face was livid, fury burning in his eyes.

But he did not move.

Because he knew she was in the enemy's hands — he dared not act rashly.

Yelü Qi drew the dagger from his waist and pressed it to Su Nian's throat.

"His Highness Prince Yu," he announced loudly, "confess your crimes. Confess, and I will let her go."

Xiao Yu stood, his voice as cold as ice. "Let her go."

"Or else what?" Yelü Qi sneered. "What can you do to me?"

Xiao Yu looked at him, a dangerous glint in his eyes.

"I will say this one more time," his voice was low and dangerous. "Let. Her. Go."

Just then, a group of guards burst into the hall and surrounded Yelü Qi.

At the same time, the sound of hoofbeats echoed from outside.

"Soldiers of Great Zhou are here! The Northern Di dogs will not escape!"

Su Nian turned to see a general in full armor leading a detachment of soldiers through the entrance.

Xiao Yu gave a cold laugh. "Did you think I did not know you were from Northern Di? I waited for you to make your move only to locate your base of operations. Now the evidence is conclusive — did you think you could escape?"

Yelü Qi's face changed.

He had not expected Xiao Yu to have seen through his identity long ago, nor that he had been prepared all along.

Xiao Yu stepped toward him, his gaze icy.

"Release her, and I will spare your life."

Yelü Qi's hand trembled slightly.

He knew the tide had turned, but he could not accept it.

Just as he hesitated, Su Nian made her move.

She opened her mouth and bit down on Yelü Qi's wrist with all her might.

"Hiss —" Yelü Qi cried out in pain, and the dagger slipped from his grasp.

Su Nian seized the opportunity to break free and ran toward Xiao Yu.

Xiao Yu caught her and pulled her behind him.

"Don't be afraid," he said softly. "I am here."

Su Nian leaned against his chest, her heart still pounding.

That was close.

She and the child had nearly lost their lives.

With a wave of his hand, Xiao Yu sent the guards forward to seize Yelü Qi.

"Take him away," Xiao Yu said coldly.

As Yelü Qi was dragged away, he laughed wildly:

"Prince Yu! Do you think this is over? The armies of Northern Di will soon trample Great Zhou into the dust! Just you wait!"

Xiao Yu ignored him. He only held Su Nian tightly.

"How could you be so foolish?" His voice was hoarse. "How could you follow a stranger of unknown origins into the palace?"

Su Nian looked up at him.

His face was pale, and his eyes held unmistakable worry and heartache.

"You were detained in the palace — how could I not come to save you?" she countered.

Xiao Yu froze.

She came to save him?

She, a mere woman, had come to save him?

"You..." He was momentarily speechless.

Su Nian smiled. "I told you — the person I care about is you. For your sake, I am willing to do anything."

Xiao Yu looked at her, his eyes reddening slightly.

He pulled her into his arms and held her tightly.

"You fool." His voice was low and tender. "Do you have any idea how worried I was about you?"

Su Nian leaned against his chest, feeling his heartbeat.

In that moment, she felt that everything was worth it.

## Chapter 15: Clarifying Hearts

After the palace banquet, Xiao Yu brought Su Nian back to Prince Yu's Residence.

The entire way, he held her hand tightly, never letting go for even a moment.

Once they arrived at the residence, he stayed by her side without leaving her for a step.

"Your Highness, you don't need to keep watching over me," Su Nian said, somewhat helpless. "I am fine."

Xiao Yu did not speak. He simply looked at her.

That gaze was so tender it hardly seemed like him — it made her feel somewhat uncomfortable.

"You had a terrible fright today," he said. "Rest early."

Su Nian nodded and was about to lie down when Xiao Yu suddenly spoke:

"Su Nian."

"Hmm?"

Xiao Yu fell silent for a moment, as though carefully choosing his words.

After a long while, he spoke. "At the palace today, you said the person you care about is me. Were you... serious?"

Su Nian's face flushed.

She had not expected him to ask this question at such a moment.

"Didn't you hear me?" she said softly.

"I want to hear it again." Xiao Yu looked at her. "Tell me yourself."

Su Nian took a deep breath, lifted her head, and met his gaze directly.

"Xiao Yu, I like you," she said. "I mean it."

A glimmer of joy flashed in Xiao Yu's eyes.

He did not say a word. He simply leaned down and kissed her gently.

It was a tender, lingering kiss.

Su Nian closed her eyes, feeling the warmth of his lips.

She had never imagined that one day she would kiss this cold-faced prince.

When the kiss ended, Xiao Yu pulled back, resting his forehead against hers.

"Su Nian," his voice was low and gentle, "I like you too."

Su Nian's heart skipped a beat.

He said... he liked her too?

Xiao Yu looked at her stunned expression, the corners of his mouth curving slightly.

"What? Are you surprised that I said I like you?"

Su Nian nodded, then shook her head.

"I thought you... you didn't like me," she said honestly. "You were always so cold to me, and you said you wanted me to leave..."

Xiao Yu sighed. "I had no choice. I am a prince — there are many things I cannot do as I please."

"I know." Su Nian said. "So I don't blame you."

Xiao Yu looked at her, a flicker of guilt in his eyes.

"I'm sorry," he said. "I have made you suffer."

Su Nian shook her head. "You don't need to apologize. As long as you like me, that is enough."

Xiao Yu pulled her into his arms and gently stroked her hair.

"From now on, I will never let you suffer again."

Su Nian leaned against his chest, her heart warm.

She had finally waited for this day.

Xiao Yu liked her.

And she liked him.

Though there were still many obstacles between them, at least for now, their hearts were together.

# Volume Four: Love and Heartbreak


## Chapter 16: Political Marriage

The good times did not last.

After the palace banquet incident, although Xiao Yu had cleared his name, the political landscape at court grew even more complex.

The Emperor was furious that a Northern Di prince had infiltrated the Capital City. He ordered a thorough investigation of all suspicious individuals at court, and for a time, everyone was on edge.

Meanwhile, Consort De — due to her connection with the Northern Di prince (Senior Matron Cui was her trusted aide) — had been confined to the palace by the Emperor and forbidden to leave.

Though Xiao Yu was not implicated, his position had actually become more difficult.

Some at court claimed that Prince Yu's achievements overshadowed the Emperor's. Others said he had colluded with Northern Di. Still others accused him of plotting rebellion...

Rumors flew everywhere, leaving him overwhelmed.

Worse still, the Emperor himself seemed to have grown wary of him.

That day, Xiao Yu came to Su Nian's courtyard, his expression grave.

Su Nian saw him and her heart skipped a beat.

"What is it?" she asked. "What happened?"

Xiao Yu was silent for a long while before speaking:

"His Majesty has commanded me to take a wife."

Su Nian's heart plummeted.

A commanded marriage?

A marriage for Xiao Yu?

"Who... who is the bride?" she asked, her voice strained.

Xiao Yu looked at her, a flash of pain in his eyes.

"The daughter of the Chancellor's household — Liu Ru Mei."

Liu Ru Mei. Su Nian remembered the name.

She was Liu Ru Yan's elder sister, the most talented woman in the Capital City, and it was said that she had admired Xiao Yu for years.

Moreover, her father was the current Chancellor — a family of great prominence, well-matched in status with Prince Yu's Residence.

Su Nian's face went pale.

She had known this day would come eventually.

Xiao Yu was a prince — how could he possibly marry a nameless orphan girl of unknown origins?

Even if he liked her, it could not withstand the cruelty of reality.

"Then... what did you say?" she asked.

Xiao Yu grasped her hand. "I refused."

Su Nian's heart leaped. "What did His Majesty say?"

Xiao Yu gave a bitter smile. "His Majesty flew into a rage. He said that by defying his command, I was being disloyal and unfilial."

Su Nian's heart sank again.

Of course...

"Your Highness," she took a deep breath, "you do not need to offend His Majesty on my account."

Xiao Yu looked at her. "I will not."

Su Nian shook her head. "Your Highness, you are a prince of the realm. You cannot jeopardize the greater scheme of things for the sake of one woman."

Xiao Yu frowned. "What do you mean?"

Su Nian lowered her head, not daring to meet his eyes.

"I... I have thought about it," her voice trembled slightly. "I cannot drag you down. If you marry the lady of the Liu family, it would be of great benefit to your political future."

Xiao Yu's expression darkened.

"Su Nian," his voice grew colder, "are you asking me to marry another woman?"

Su Nian bit her lip. "I don't want that. But... but I have no choice."

She raised her head, tears pooling in her eyes.

"I am a transmigrator. I have no identity, no way to help you. I don't even know why I came to this world..."

Her voice grew lower and lower, until it was barely audible.

"I only bring you trouble. It would be better to—"

"Enough!" Xiao Yu cut her off, his voice tight with suppressed fury.

Su Nian startled and looked up at him.

Xiao Yu took a deep breath, forcing down his anger.

"Su Nian, I am going to ask you one question, and I want you to answer honestly."

Su Nian nodded.

Xiao Yu looked at her, enunciating each word carefully: "Do you want me to marry someone else?"

Su Nian froze.

Did she... want that?

The answer, of course, was no.

She loved Xiao Yu. She did not want any other woman near him.

But what right did she have to stop him?

"N... no," she said at last, honestly.

Xiao Yu's gaze softened.

"Then that is enough," he said. "I will not marry anyone else."

Su Nian shook her head. "But what about His Majesty?"

Xiao Yu fell silent for a moment. "I will find a way."

Su Nian looked at him, not knowing what to say.

She knew that when he said "find a way," it would likely come at a great cost.

---


## Chapter 17: Misunderstandings Abound

The matter of the commanded marriage dragged on for days.

Xiao Yu was busy from dawn to dusk — either being summoned to the palace to see the Emperor or handling official duties.

Su Nian stayed alone in her courtyard, her heart in turmoil.

She did not know whether Xiao Yu could persuade the Emperor, or what would happen next.

That day, Liu Ru Yan suddenly appeared.

"Miss Su," she smiled with hidden meaning. "It has been a while."

Su Nian looked at her, her guard going up.

Liu Ru Yan had been confined for quite some time — how was she out already?

"What brings Concubine Liu to my humble quarters?" she asked coolly.

Liu Ru Yan covered her mouth with a delicate laugh. "I came to see you, of course. After all, you are His Highness's favorite now."

Su Nian frowned. "Say what you have to say, Concubine."

Liu Ru Yan's smile deepened. "Miss Su is as straightforward as ever. Very well — I will not beat around the bush."

She leaned closer to Su Nian and lowered her voice. "Miss Su, did you know that my elder sister is to marry into the residence?"

Su Nian's face changed.

Liu Ru Yan continued, "His Majesty commanded the marriage, and His Highness defied the decree. His Majesty was already furious. But later, I heard that His Highness agreed to a condition, and His Majesty reluctantly agreed to postpone the wedding."

Su Nian's heart tightened. "What condition?"

Liu Ru Yan smiled. "His Highness agreed... to send Miss Su out of the Capital City."

Su Nian froze.

What?

Xiao Yu had agreed to send her away?

"Miss Su," Liu Ru Yan looked at her, a mocking gleam in her eyes. "Did you truly believe His Highness cared for you? He was merely infatuated with something new. Once my sister enters the household, he will naturally realize who is best suited for him."

Su Nian's face went deathly pale.

She did not believe it. She did not believe Xiao Yu would do such a thing.

But... if it were not true, how would Liu Ru Yan know?

"No... impossible," she murmured.

Liu Ru Yan gave a cold smile. "Believe it or not. But I heard that His Highness has already made the arrangements — you will be sent out of the Capital City tomorrow."

With that, she turned and left, leaving Su Nian standing there in stunned silence.

Su Nian sat alone in her room, her mind in chaos.

Xiao Yu had agreed to send her away?

Was it true?

She wanted to go find Xiao Yu and demand the truth, but she did not dare.

What if it was real?

What if he truly did want to send her away?

She did not know what to do.

---


## Chapter 18: Rival Schemes

That night, Su Nian did not sleep at all.

She thought about many things.

Was Xiao Yu's kindness to her genuine?

Or was it only because of the child she carried?

If one day the child was born, would he still treat her well?

She did not know.

She only knew that she was afraid.

Afraid that all of this was just a dream. Afraid that Xiao Yu was merely using her. Afraid that she would give everything, only to end up with nothing.

At dawn, she finally made her decision.

Since he was going to send her away, she would leave on her own terms.

Rather than being driven out, she would depart with dignity.

She packed her belongings, left a letter, and quietly slipped out of the residence.

The letter read:

"Xiao Yu, I have left. I will take good care of the child. You need not worry."

That was all. No farewell, no explanation.

She was afraid that if she wrote even one more word, she would not be able to hold back her tears.

---

By the time Xiao Yu discovered Su Nian was gone, it was already noon.

He rushed into her room and saw the empty space. He stood there, stunned.

"Where is she?" He grabbed Xiao Cui and demanded harshly, "Where is Su Nian?"

Xiao Cui was sobbing. "Miss... Miss has gone! She left a letter and just left!"

Xiao Yu snatched the letter. After reading it, his face turned livid.

"Damn it!" He crumpled the letter into a ball. "Who told her I was going to send her away?!"

Xiao Cui stammered through her tears. "It... it was Concubine Liu. Concubine Liu came yesterday and said... said something about His Majesty's marriage decree, and that His Highness was going to send Miss away..."

A murderous glint flashed in Xiao Yu's eyes.

Liu Ru Yan!

Her again!

He spun around, about to go confront Liu Ru Yan, but the steward stopped him.

"Your Highness, please calm yourself! The most important thing right now is to find Miss Su! Miss Su is pregnant — it is far too dangerous for her to be out there alone!"

Xiao Yu forced himself to calm down.

The steward was right. Now was not the time to assign blame.

He took a deep breath and issued his orders. "Send men out to search immediately! Turn the entire Capital City upside down if you must — but find her!"

"Yes!"

Xiao Yu stood in the empty room, feeling as though something had been hollowed out of his chest.

Su Nian...

How could she do this?

He had told her he would never let her suffer. He had told her he liked her.

Why did she not trust him?

Why did she leave?

He did not know.

He only knew that he could not lose her.

---


## Chapter 19: Heartbroken Departure

After leaving the residence, Su Nian wandered the streets alone.

She did not know where to go.

She had no money, no friends, and did not even understand the rules of this world.

As she walked, she felt a pain in her belly.

She found a quiet spot and sat down, clutching her stomach.

Baby... was the baby protesting?

She gently stroked her belly and whispered, "I'm sorry, little one. Your mother has burdened you."

Her tears fell.

She missed Xiao Yu.

Though she had been gone for less than a day, she already longed for him.

She missed his smile, his embrace, the tenderness in his voice when he said "I like you."

But she could not go back.

If he truly wanted to send her away, returning would only bring her humiliation.

She would rather suffer alone than see him look at her with disdain.

Just then, a gentle voice came from behind her.

"Young lady? Is that you?"

Su Nian turned to see an elderly woman standing a short distance away.

The woman was about fifty years old, dressed in simple homespun cloth, with a kind and amiable face.

"Madam, I think you have mistaken me for someone else," Su Nian said.

The old woman came closer and studied her face carefully. "No mistake. You are the young lady who saved my grandson at the temple fair, aren't you?"

Su Nian paused, then remembered.

A few days earlier, when she had gone out shopping, she had seen a little boy struck by a horse carriage. She had rushed forward to help, then left afterward.

She had not expected the boy's grandmother to recognize her.

"Madam," Su Nian managed a weak smile. "It's you."

The old woman took her hand. "Young lady, what has happened to you? You look so pale. Have you run into some trouble?"

Su Nian shook her head and tried to pull her hand away.

But the old woman would not let go. "Young lady, don't hide it from me. Are you in some kind of difficulty? Tell me — let me help you!"

Su Nian looked into her concerned eyes, and her heart ached.

There were still good people in this world.

"Madam," she finally broke down in tears. "I... I have nowhere to go..."

---


## Chapter 20: Prince Chases His Wife

The old woman brought Su Nian back to her home.

Her house was in a small village on the outskirts of the city. Though not wealthy, it was clean and tidy.

"Stay here and rest easy," the old woman said. "We can figure everything out later."

Su Nian was endlessly grateful and thanked her repeatedly.

She settled in at the old woman's home.

The old woman's son and daughter-in-law were ordinary farmers, and they treated her kindly.

Her belly grew larger with each passing day, and the days were relatively peaceful.

But at night, she often thought of Xiao Yu.

She wondered how he was doing now.

She wondered whether he was looking for her.

She wondered how things stood with his marriage to Liu Ru Mei...

She dared not think about it.

Whenever she thought of him, her heart ached as though being cut by a knife.

---

On Xiao Yu's end, he had practically lost his mind.

He had sent everyone he had to search for Su Nian.

Shadow guards, palace guards, household servants — they had practically turned the entire Great Zhou Dynasty upside down.

But Su Nian seemed to have vanished into thin air. No matter how hard they searched, they could not find her.

He could not sleep at night.

Every time he closed his eyes, he saw Su Nian's face.

Her crying, her smiling, her calling his name...

He missed her. He missed her so much it hurt.

He hated himself.

He hated himself for failing to protect her. He hated himself for letting her suffer. He hated himself for not telling her everything sooner.

That day, Liu Ru Yan had told her that His Majesty wanted to send Su Nian away. He had wanted to argue, wanted to tell her the truth.

But before he could say a word, she was gone.

She must have misunderstood.

She must have thought he truly intended to send her away.

And so she left.

Xiao Yu did not know who to blame.

Should he blame Liu Ru Yan for sowing discord? Should he blame Su Nian for not trusting him? Or should he blame himself for being useless?

He only knew one thing — he had to find her.

Even if he had to search every corner of the Great Zhou Dynasty, he would find her.

---

*(End of Volume Four)*

# Volume Five: Reunion


## Chapter 21: Living Alone

Time passed swiftly.

In the blink of an eye, Su Nian had been living in the small village for three months.

Her belly had grown quite large; she would give birth any day now.

The old woman treated her kindly, caring for her as if she were her own daughter.

The villagers, too, knew she was a pitiful soul and looked after her well.

Su Nian lived peacefully here.

Though she still thought of Xiao Yu from time to time, the pain was no longer as sharp as before.

Time was, indeed, the best medicine.

One day, as she was basking in the sun in the courtyard, the village chief arrived unexpectedly.

"Miss Su," the village chief said, "someone is looking for you."

Su Nian froze. "Who?"

The village chief pointed toward the gate. "He says his surname is Xiao."

Su Nian's heart leaped.

Surname Xiao?

Could it be...

She rose to her feet and walked toward the entrance.

And then she saw Xiao Yu.

He stood outside the courtyard gate, travel-worn and dusty.

His face was thinner than before, with dark circles beneath his eyes. He looked altogether haggard and drawn.

But his gaze was still bright, still carrying that familiar tenderness she knew so well.

Separated only by the courtyard gate, their eyes met.

Su Nian stood frozen, unsure of what to say.

Xiao Yu looked at her, his eyes reddening slightly.

"Su Nian," his voice was hoarse. "I found you."

---


## Chapter 22: Difficult Childbirth

Xiao Yu had found Su Nian, but he did not take her away.

Because he knew she was still angry.

He explained to her what had happened that day.

Everything Liu Ru Yan had told her that day had been lies.

He had indeed agreed to a condition set by the Emperor, but the condition was not to send Su Nian away — it was to send Liu Ru Yan away.

Liu Ru Yan had spread rumors and framed Su Nian; he would no longer tolerate her.

And the words Su Nian had overheard about "sending her away" had been deliberately fabricated by Liu Ru Yan to deceive her.

Su Nian listened, and tears streamed down her face.

So... so she had misunderstood.

She had thought Xiao Yu no longer wanted her, and that was why she had left.

But the truth was, Xiao Yu had never once considered abandoning her.

"I'm sorry," she sobbed. "It was my fault. I shouldn't have doubted you."

Xiao Yu pulled her into his arms, gently stroking her hair.

"Don't blame yourself. I didn't explain things clearly to you," he said. "From now on, no matter what happens, I will tell you everything. Don't let your imagination run wild on your own."

Su Nian nodded, leaning against his chest.

At this moment, she felt like the happiest person in the world.

---

Xiao Yu did not take Su Nian away.

Because she was about to give birth and was in no condition to travel long distances.

He stayed behind, remaining by her side.

The villagers were astonished — they had never expected Prince Yu to come to their little village in person.

But they asked no questions, quietly accepting this distinguished guest.

Su Nian's delivery went smoothly.

On a bright, sunlit morning, she gave birth to a healthy baby boy.

The child cried lustily at birth — plump, fair, and perfectly healthy.

Xiao Yu held the baby, his eyes reddening.

"Su Nian," he leaned down and kissed her forehead. "Thank you. You've been through so much."

Su Nian smiled weakly. "Give him a name."

Xiao Yu thought for a moment. "Let's call him Xiao Nian. Nian, as in *nian* — to long for, to remember. To remind me never to forget you."

Su Nian's tears fell once more.

Xiao Nian.

The child of Xiao Yu and Su Nian.

What a beautiful name.

---


## Chapter 23: Prince's Investigation

After Su Nian gave birth, Xiao Yu began setting things right in the Capital City.

He uncovered Liu Ru Yan's crimes and sent her to the family temple for penance.

He exposed Consort De's schemes, deepening the Emperor's disgust toward her.

He dismantled the Northern Di spy network within the Great Zhou Dynasty, bringing all those involved to justice.

And he himself finally cleared his name of all suspicion, regaining the Emperor's trust.

Once the dust had settled, Xiao Yu brought Su Nian and their child back to the Capital City.

Consort De, with irrefutable evidence of her collusion with the Northern Di, was stripped of her title by the Emperor, reduced to a commoner, and cast into the Cold Palace.

Liu Ru Yan, for framing Su Nian, was sent to the family temple to become a nun.

Liu Ru Mei, too, was caught in the fallout — no longer the daughter of the Prime Minister's household. It was said that upon learning the truth, she fell gravely ill and later took Buddhist vows, spending the rest of her days in devotion before the altar.

As for Xiao Yu, having rendered great service during this affair, the Emperor granted him the title of Regent Prince, second in authority only to the Emperor himself.

For a time, Prince Yu's Residence was the envy of all.

But Xiao Yu cared nothing for such things.

He cared only about Su Nian and their child.

---


## Chapter 24: Truth Revealed

After returning to the Capital City, Xiao Yu did something momentous.

He petitioned the Emperor for a decree to take Su Nian as his Princess Consort.

The Emperor hesitated.

Su Nian was, after all, a woman of unknown origins — no family name, no background to speak of.

For the Regent Prince to take such a woman as his primary wife would surely invite criticism.

But Xiao Yu stood firm.

He said that though Su Nian was a transmigrator, she was kind-hearted and immensely talented. She had saved him, and she had saved this nation. A woman like her was worth devoting his entire life to protecting.

The Emperor was moved and finally relented.

But before the formal wedding, there was one more thing Xiao Yu needed to do.

He uncovered the secret of Su Nian's origins.

It turned out that the original owner of Su Nian's body was a descendant of the former dynasty's princess.

When the old dynasty had fallen, the princess had narrowly escaped, changing her name and living in hiding among the common people. The mother of this body was the princess's own great-granddaughter.

So, strictly speaking, Su Nian — or rather, the original owner of the body — carried the royal bloodline of the former dynasty.

But the old dynasty had fallen decades ago; her identity no longer held any political significance.

Xiao Yu, however, cared nothing about that.

He simply wanted Su Nian to have a proper identity, so that their marriage would be beyond reproach.

When Su Nian learned of this, she was filled with deep emotion.

She had never imagined that the origins of this body were so complex.

But she cared nothing for any princess's bloodline. She was simply grateful to have met Xiao Yu, and to be with him.

---


## Chapter 25: Reunion and Recognition

On the day of the grand wedding, the entire Capital City was in an uproar.

Prince Yu, Xiao Yu, was taking a wife. It was said the bride was a woman of mysterious origins who had once saved the Prince's life.

Though many disapproved of the match, no one dared voice their objections out of respect for Prince Yu.

Dressed in the phoenix crown and ceremonial robes, Su Nian sat inside the bridal palanquin, her heart a flutter of nerves and excitement.

She was about to marry Xiao Yu.

She was about to become the Princess Consort.

This was something she would never have dared dream of before she transmigrated.

When the palanquin arrived at Prince Yu's Residence, Xiao Yu was waiting at the entrance to receive her personally.

He wore a grand crimson wedding gown, and a rare smile graced his handsome face.

As Su Nian stepped out of the palanquin, he stepped forward and took her hand.

"Don't be afraid," he murmured. "I am here."

Su Nian looked at him, her eyes reddening.

"Mm," she said. "I know."

Hand in hand, they walked together into the Residence.

The guests watched in stunned silence.

The Prince and his Princess Consort — they truly looked like a perfect match.

---


## Chapter 26: Renewed Connection

On their wedding night, Xiao Yu and Su Nian could finally speak freely to one another.

Xiao Yu held Su Nian close, pressing gentle kisses to her forehead.

"Su Nian, I have finally made you mine."

Su Nian smiled. "Xiao Yu, I have finally become your wife."

Xiao Yu gazed at her, his eyes brimming with tenderness.

"From now on, the three of us — we will never be apart again."

Su Nian nodded. "Never apart. Not ever."

They looked at each other and smiled, then held each other tightly.

At this moment, all the suffering they had endured was worth it.

They had been through misunderstanding, separation, heartbreak, and pain...

But in the end, they were together.

Fate had brought them together, made them fall in love, and now they would remain together forever.

---

*(End of Volume Five)*

# Bonus: Happy Ending


## Bonus 1: Family of Three

Five years later.

In the gardens of Prince Yu's Residence, a cherubic little boy was chasing after butterflies.

"Mother! Mother! Look! Such a pretty butterfly!" Little Xiao Nian came running up to Su Nian, holding the butterfly aloft.

Su Nian sat in the pavilion, smiling gently. "Nian'er is so clever — you caught a butterfly!"

Xiao Yu walked over and scooped their son up in his arms. "Are you bothering your mother again?"

Little Xiao Nian giggled. "Father, I'm not bothering Mother! I'm making her happy!"

Xiao Yu affectionately pinched his little nose. "You little rascal."

Su Nian watched father and son together, her heart overflowing with happiness.

Five years.

It had been five years since she and Xiao Yu were married.

In these five years, they had been through so much.

She had gone from being a transmigrator to the Princess Consort, from a lone orphan to the mother of two children.

She had borne a son and a daughter, completing the word for "good fortune."

Her son was named Xiao Nian, now four years old. Her daughter was named Xiao Qing, who had just turned one.

Xiao Yu had been wonderful to her — his heart had never wavered.

Every day, he dined with her, strolled with her, and watched the children by her side.

He was a good husband, and a good father.

She felt like the happiest woman in the world.

---


## Bonus 2: The Prince Chases His Wife (Sweet Daily Life)

"Where is the Princess Consort?" Xiao Yu asked the moment he returned to the Residence.

The steward bowed and replied, "Your Highness, the Princess Consort is in the kitchen making pastries."

Xiao Yu raised an eyebrow. "Making pastries again?"

The steward smiled wryly. "Her Ladyship said she wanted to try a new recipe and have the servants taste it."

Xiao Yu shook his head and headed toward the kitchen.

Ever since Su Nian became pregnant, she had been endlessly experimenting with food.

One moment she wanted to make cake, the next cookies, the next milk tea...

Though most of her attempts ended in failure, she never tired of trying.

When Xiao Yu reached the kitchen doorway, he found Su Nian busy at work inside.

She wore a simple dress, her hair carelessly pinned up, with flour dusted across her face.

Xiao Yu's heart softened at the sight.

This woman — she looked beautiful no matter what.

"Princess Consort," he stepped inside. "I'm home."

Su Nian turned around, and her face lit up at the sight of him. "You're back! Try my new pastry!"

Xiao Yu glanced at the "pastry" on the table, the corner of his mouth twitching.

That dark, lumpy mass — was it really edible?

Su Nian seemed to sense his doubt and laughed. "Don't worry, it's a chocolate cake. It melts in your mouth — it's delicious!"

Xiao Yu hesitated for a moment, then picked up a piece and tasted it.

The flavor was... unexpectedly good.

"Well?" Su Nian looked at him expectantly.

Xiao Yu nodded. "Not bad."

Su Nian jumped for joy. "Wonderful! I finally succeeded!"

Xiao Yu looked at her childlike excitement and couldn't help but laugh.

He stepped forward and pulled her into his arms.

"Princess Consort, even if your pastries were terrible, I would still love them."

Su Nian looked up at him. "Why?"

Xiao Yu leaned down and pressed a gentle kiss to her lips.

"Because you made them."

Su Nian's cheeks flushed, and her heart raced.

This man — even after all these years of marriage, he still knew exactly how to make her melt.

"Xiao Yu," she whispered, "I love you."

A flash of surprise crossed Xiao Yu's eyes.

In all five years of their marriage, Su Nian rarely said those three words. She always expressed her feelings with quiet restraint, seldom so directly.

"Say it again," he said.

Su Nian smiled. "I love you, Xiao Yu."

Xiao Yu pulled her close and kissed her deeply.

The maidservants in the kitchen quietly slipped out, covering their mouths to stifle their giggles.

The Prince and the Princess Consort were truly devoted to each other — it was enough to make anyone envious.
