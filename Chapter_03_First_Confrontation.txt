## Chapter 3: First Confrontation

Early the next morning, Su Nian dressed up carefully and followed Xiao Cui to the study.

The study of the Yu Prince's Residence was on the east side of the main courtyard, a separate small courtyard. Two guards stood at the courtyard entrance; when they saw her coming, they immediately saluted and let her pass.

Su Nian took a deep breath and stepped in.

The study was large, simply yet elegantly furnished. Three walls were lined with bookshelves filled with various books. In the center was a large rosewood desk, piled with some official documents.

Xiao Yu was sitting behind the desk, holding a book and reading.

Hearing the footsteps, he looked up.

"Sit."

Su Nian sat down in the chair below as instructed, her back straight, hands properly placed on her knees.

Xiao Yu put down the book, his gaze falling on her.

"Your name is Su Nian?"

"Yes."

"How old are you this year?"

"...Seventeen." Su Nian answered uncertainly. This was what Xiao Cui had told her.

"Where are you from?"

"Outside the capital."

"Are there any relatives left in your family?"

"No, my parents died early."

Xiao Yu's eyes flickered slightly: "Do you know who those people were that day, the ones hunting you?"

Su Nian's heart tightened, and she shook her head: "I don't remember."

"Really don't remember?"

Xiao Yu's tone carried a bit of meaningful depth.

Su Nian braced herself and nodded: "This commoner hit my head and can't remember many things."

Xiao Yu stared at her for a long time, making her feel like she was sitting on pins and needles.

Just when she was about to break, he finally withdrew his gaze: "Forget it, this prince has already sent people to investigate. Until it's cleared up, you can stay in the residence."

Su Nian breathed a sigh of relief: "Thank you, Your Highness."

"No need to thank this prince." Xiao Yu said indifferently, "This prince saved you because there's something this prince wants from you."

Su Nian was stunned: "What thing?"

Xiao Yu didn't answer, but instead asked: "Do you know how to read?"

"...A little." Su Nian answered cautiously. She was a university student in her previous life, so she could read. But she didn't know if the original owner of this body could read or not.

"Then help this prince copy a few books." Xiao Yu stood up, took out several volumes from the bookshelf, and placed them before her, "When you're done copying, this prince will let you leave."

Su Nian looked down; they were several thick account books.

It seemed this Prince Yu wanted her to be free labor.

But this was good too. At least she had a legitimate reason to stay in the residence, and she could take the opportunity to learn about the writing and system of this world.

"This commoner obeys."

Xiao Yu nodded, about to say something, when a commotion suddenly came from outside the door.

"Your Highness! Your Highness!" A sharp female voice approached from afar, "I heard Your Highness brought a woman back to the residence. I've come to see!"

Su Nian turned to see a woman in gorgeous clothes walking in quickly.

The woman was about twenty years old, strikingly beautiful, with almond-shaped eyes that glanced around captivatingly. She wore a pink embroidered long dress, with pearls and jade in her hair. As she walked, her waist swayed gently, looking enchanting.

Xiao Yu's brow furrowed slightly: "Concubine Liu, this is the study. What are you doing here?"

Su Nian suddenly realized.

So she was Prince Yu's concubine. No wonder she hurried over as soon as she heard the prince had brought a woman back.

It seemed this Prince Yu had quite good luck with women.

Liu Ruoyan walked into the study, her gaze immediately falling on Su Nian, sizing her up from head to toe.

"So this is the woman His Highness brought back from outside?" She covered her lips with a smile, her voice carrying a bit of contempt, "She doesn't look like much. Skinny and small, not pretty at all."

Su Nian felt a bit unhappy inside, but didn't show it on her face.

She didn't know what background this concubine had; it probably wasn't good to offend her rashly.

"Concubine Liu is joking." She slightly bowed, "This commoner is just staying temporarily in the residence to recover from injuries, not daring to compare with Concubine Liu."

Liu Ruoyan heard this, and the smile on her face deepened: "At least you know your place."

She turned to Xiao Yu, acting spoiled: "Your Highness, I heard this woman is of unknown origins. How can you let someone of unknown origins live in the residence? What if she's some dangerous person..."

"Enough." Xiao Yu interrupted her, his voice a few degrees colder, "This prince's affairs are not for you to comment on."

Liu Ruoyan's smile froze on her face, a hurt look flashing in her eyes.

"Your Highness..."

"Get out." Xiao Yu's tone brooked no argument.

Liu Ruoyan bit her lip, glanced at Su Nian, and turned to leave resentfully.

Su Nian watched her departing figure, feeling somewhat conflicted inside.

This Concubine Liu seemed to have genuine feelings for Xiao Yu. Unfortunately, the falling flowers have intent, but the flowing water is heartless; Xiao Yu obviously had no feelings for her.

"Seen enough?" Xiao Yu's voice brought her back to reality.

Su Nian withdrew her gaze and lowered her eyes: "This commoner knows her mistake."

Xiao Yu didn't say anything more, just waved his hand: "Take the account books and go. Come back to report when you're done copying."

Su Nian picked up those thick account books, curtsied, and turned to leave.

Walking out of the study, she finally let out a long breath.

This Prince Yu's aura was truly terrifying. He didn't even say anything harsh, yet she broke out in a cold sweat.

But that Liu Ruoyan was obviously not someone to be trifled with. Living in the residence, she would probably be targeted by her frequently in the future.

Forget it, take it one step at a time.

Now the most important thing was to familiarize herself with the writing and system of this world, and by the way, investigate the origins of those men in black.
