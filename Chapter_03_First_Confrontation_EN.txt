## Chapter 3: First Confrontation

Early the next morning, Su Nian dressed carefully and followed Xiao Cui to the prince's study.

The study of Prince Yu's Residence was located on the eastern side of the main compound, in an independent courtyard of its own. Two guards stood at the gate; when they saw her approach, they bowed and let her pass.

Su Nian drew a deep breath and stepped inside.

The study was large, its decor simple yet refined. Bookshelves lined three walls, filled with volumes of every description. At the center stood a massive rosewood desk, atop which lay several official documents.

Xiao Yu sat behind the desk, reading a book.

At the sound of her footsteps, he looked up.

"Sit."

Su Nian obeyed, taking the chair at the lower end. She sat with her back straight and her hands folded neatly on her knees.

Xiao Yu set down his book and fixed his gaze upon her.

"Your name is Su Nian?"

"Yes."

"How old are you?"

"...Seventeen." Su Nian answered uncertainly. This was what Xiao Cui had told her.

"Where are you from?"

"The outskirts of the Capital City."

"Do you have any living family?"

"No. My parents died when I was young."

Xiao Yu's gaze flickered. "The people who pursued you that day — do you know who they were?"

Su Nian's heart tightened. She shook her head. "I don't remember."

"You truly don't remember?"

There was a note of significance in Xiao Yu's tone.

Su Nian gritted her teeth and nodded. "I struck my head. Many things are a blank to me."

Xiao Yu stared at her for a long while, making her feel as though she were sitting on pins and needles.

Just as she was about to crack under the pressure, he finally looked away. "Very well. I have already sent men to investigate. Until the matter is cleared up, you will remain at the residence."

Su Nian breathed a sigh of relief. "Thank you, Your Highness."

"Do not thank me." Xiao Yu's tone was indifferent. "I saved you because there is something I want from you."

Su Nian blinked. "What is that?"

Xiao Yu did not answer. Instead, he asked, "Can you read and write?"

"...A little." Su Nian replied cautiously. In her previous life, she had been a university graduate — of course she could read. But she did not know whether the original owner of this body had been literate.

"Then help me copy some ledgers." Xiao Yu rose, pulled several thick volumes from the shelves, and set them before her. "Once you have finished copying them, I will let you leave."

Su Nian looked down. They were hefty account books.

It seemed the prince intended to put her to work as free labor.

Still, this was not entirely unwelcome. At least it gave her a legitimate reason to remain at the residence, and she could use the opportunity to familiarize herself with this world's writing and systems.

"I will do as you command."

Xiao Yu nodded. He was about to say something more when a commotion erupted outside.

"Your Highness! Your Highness!" A sharp female voice drew nearer. "I heard that Your Highness has brought a woman back to the residence — I came to see for myself!"

Su Nian turned to look. A gorgeously dressed woman was sweeping into the study.

She was in her early twenties, strikingly beautiful, with almond eyes that sparkled with vivacity. She wore a pink embroidered gown, her hair adorned with pearls and jade, and swayed gently as she walked — the very picture of allure.

Xiao Yu's brow creased. "Concubine Liu, this is my study. What are you doing here?"

Su Nian understood at once.

So this was the prince's concubine. No wonder she had rushed over the moment she heard he had brought a woman home.

It seemed Prince Yu was rather popular with the ladies.

Liu Ru Yan entered the study and her gaze immediately fell on Su Nian, sweeping over her from head to toe.

"So this is the woman His Highness brought back from outside?" She covered her lips with a delicate smile, her voice laced with contempt. "She doesn't look like much. So thin and small — not pretty at all."

Su Nian felt a flicker of irritation but kept her expression neutral.

She did not know what influence this concubine wielded, and provoking her rashly would be unwise.

"Concubine Liu is too kind." She gave a slight bow. "I am merely staying at the residence to recover from my injuries. I could never compare to you, my lady."

Liu Ru Yan's smile deepened. "At least you know your place."

She turned to Xiao Yu and adopted a coaxing tone. "Your Highness, I heard that this woman's origins are unknown. How can you let someone of unclear background stay in the residence? What if she is a dangerous person..."

"Enough." Xiao Yu cut her off, his voice dropping several degrees. "My affairs are not yours to comment on."

Liu Ru Yan's smile froze. A flash of hurt crossed her eyes.

"Your Highness..."

"Leave." His tone brooked no argument.

Liu Ru Yan bit her lip, shot Su Nian a venomous glare, and turned on her heel.

Su Nian watched her retreating figure, her feelings mixed.

It seemed Concubine Liu genuinely had feelings for Xiao Yu. But the falling blossom harbored longing while the flowing water remained indifferent — Xiao Yu clearly had no feelings for her whatsoever.

"Have you seen enough?" Xiao Yu's voice pulled her back to reality.

Su Nian averted her gaze and lowered her eyes. "I apologize, Your Highness."

Xiao Yu said nothing further. He merely waved his hand. "Take the ledgers and go. Return when you have finished copying them."

Su Nian gathered the thick volumes into her arms, curtsied, and turned to leave.

Only after stepping out of the study did she allow herself a long exhale.

Prince Yu's presence was truly formidable. He had not said a single harsh word, yet she was drenched in cold sweat.

But Concubine Liu was clearly no easy opponent either. Living in the residence, Su Nian would likely face her hostility again and again.

Never mind — she would cross each bridge when she came to it.

For now, the most important thing was to familiarize herself with this world's writing and institutions, and to investigate the origins of those men in black along the way.
