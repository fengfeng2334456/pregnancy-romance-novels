## Chapter 19: Heartbroken Departure

After leaving the residence, Su Nian wandered the streets alone.

She did not know where to go.

She had no money, no friends, and did not even understand the rules of this world.

With her belly over six months along, she walked with great difficulty. Her big belly was heavy and cumbersome, each step requiring tremendous effort. She supported her waist with one hand and cradled her belly with the other, needing to stop and rest after only a few steps.

Passersby cast sidelong glances, some pointing and whispering, others looking at her with sympathy.

"Why is that young lady alone? With such a big belly, and no one to care for her?"

"She looks so pitiful..."

Su Nian lowered her head, not daring to meet anyone's eyes.

She felt unprecedented shame and helplessness.

Once, she had been Prince Yu's most favored woman, with servants attending to her wherever she went. But now, she was like a beggar, wandering the streets with her big belly.

She walked to a corner and slowly sat down, leaning against the wall.

Her big belly pressed against her, making it uncomfortable even to sit. She could only lean to one side, one hand propping herself against the wall, the other protecting her belly.

"Little one," she whispered. "Mother is sorry... for making you suffer with Mother..."

A gentle fetal movement came from within, as if comforting her.

Su Nian's tears fell again.

She missed Xiao Yu.

Though she had been gone for less than a day, she already longed for him.

She missed his smile, his embrace, the tenderness in his voice when he said "I like you."

But she could not go back.

If he truly wanted to send her away, returning would only bring her humiliation.

She would rather suffer alone than see him look at her with disdain.

---

After resting for a while, Su Nian struggled to stand up again.

With her big belly, she supported herself against the wall and walked forward step by step.

Her feet were swollen, her shoes pinching painfully. Her waist ached terribly, as if someone had struck it with a hammer.

But she could not stop.

She had to find a place to stay.

She walked for a long time, so long that her legs went numb and her belly began to ache dully.

"Little one..." She covered her belly with one hand and supported herself against the wall with the other, her forehead covered in cold sweat. "You... don't fuss... Mother... Mother can't walk anymore..."

A violent fetal movement came from within, as if protesting her hardship.

Su Nian bit her lip, enduring the pain as she continued forward.

She couldn't stop.

She couldn't collapse here.

---

Finally, she reached the city gate.

The soldiers guarding the gate were somewhat surprised to see her standing alone with such a large belly.

"Young lady, where are you going?" one soldier asked.

Su Nian shook her head. "I... I don't know..."

She truly didn't know.

She only wanted to leave this place, to leave the place that had broken her heart.

As for where to go, she really hadn't thought about it.

The soldier looked at her wretched state and sighed. "Young lady, in your condition, how far can you go? Perhaps you should go back."

"Go back?" Su Nian gave a bitter smile. "I... I have nowhere to go back to..."

As she spoke, her tears fell again.

The soldiers looked at each other, unsure of what to do.

Just then, a gentle voice came from behind her.

"Young lady? Is that you?"

Su Nian turned to see an elderly woman standing a short distance away.

The woman was about fifty years old, dressed in simple homespun cloth, with a kind and amiable face.

"Madam, I think you have mistaken me for someone else," Su Nian said.

The old woman came closer and studied her face carefully. "No mistake. You are the young lady who saved my grandson at the temple fair, aren't you?"

Su Nian paused, then remembered.

A few days earlier, when she had gone out shopping, she had seen a little boy struck by a horse carriage. She had rushed forward to help, then left afterward.

She had not expected the boy's grandmother to recognize her.

"Madam," Su Nian managed a weak smile. "It's you."

The old woman took her hand. "Young lady, what has happened to you? You look so pale. Have you run into some trouble?"

Su Nian shook her head and tried to pull her hand away.

But the old woman would not let go. "Young lady, don't hide it from me. Are you in some kind of difficulty? Tell me — let me help you!"

Su Nian looked into her concerned eyes, and her heart ached.

There were still good people in this world.

"Madam," she finally broke down in tears. "I... I have nowhere to go..."

---

The old woman brought Su Nian back to her home.

Her house was in a small village on the outskirts of the city. Though simple, it was clean and tidy.

"Stay here and rest easy," the old woman said. "We can figure everything out later."

Su Nian was endlessly grateful and thanked her repeatedly.

She settled in at the old woman's home.

The old woman's son and daughter-in-law were ordinary farmers, and they treated her kindly.

But her belly grew larger with each passing day, and movement became increasingly difficult.

With her belly now seven months along, she couldn't even bend over. Every time she wanted to pick something up from the ground, she had to squat down with great difficulty, one hand supporting her belly, the other reaching out.

Sleeping also became a problem.

Her big belly pressed on her, making it hard to breathe. She could only lie on her side, with a pillow behind her waist for support. Even so, she often woke in the middle of the night, her back and waist aching so much she couldn't sleep.

"Young lady," the old woman said, looking at her suffering with心疼. "Your belly is getting bigger and bigger. You must be careful."

"I know," Su Nian said, touching her belly with a bitter smile. "Thank you for taking me in."

"Silly child," the old woman patted her hand. "You saved my grandson, so you are a benefactor to our family. Stay here and rest. We'll talk again after the child is born."

Su Nian nodded, her eyes reddening.

In this world, to encounter such good people was truly fortunate.

---

Days passed, and Su Nian's belly grew larger and larger.

She was already over eight months along, her belly protruding like a large watermelon, requiring someone's help just to walk.

She could only pace slowly in the courtyard each day to stretch her muscles.

Her big belly made movement inconvenient. Even putting on shoes required help. She couldn't bend down, so she could only sit at the edge of the bed, lift her foot, and struggle to reach her shoes.

"Young lady, let me help you." The old woman's daughter-in-law hurried over when she saw her struggling.

"Thank you..." Su Nian said, embarrassed.

"What are you thanking me for?" The daughter-in-law smiled. "You're carrying a child, so of course you should be cared for."

Su Nian looked down at her big belly, her heart filled with mixed emotions.

She thought of Xiao Yu.

If he were here, he would surely support her carefully, afraid that she might fall or get hurt.

But now, she had to endure all of this alone.

---

That night, Su Nian lay in bed, tossing and turning.

Her big belly pressed on her, making it hard to breathe. Her waist ached terribly.

She lay on her side, one hand propping herself up on the bed, the other gently stroking her belly.

"Little one," she whispered. "Your father... what is he doing now?"

A fetal movement came from within, as if responding to her.

Su Nian's tears silently fell.

"Little one, Mother misses him..." she choked out. "But... but Mother can't go back..."

She stroked her belly, feeling the movements of the child inside, her heart filled with conflict and pain.

She loved Xiao Yu.

But she was also afraid.

Afraid that if she went back, she would only see his cold gaze.

Afraid that he truly saw her only as a tool for bearing children.

Afraid that she would give everything, only to end up with nothing.

"Little one," she whispered. "No matter what happens, Mother will protect you."

She closed her eyes, guarding the child in her belly through the long night alone.
