## Chapter 2: Temporary Stay

Over the following days, Su Nian remained in bed, recovering from her injuries.

Xiao Cui brought her medicine and meals on time each day, and chatted with her about life in the residence.

Gradually, she gained a preliminary understanding of this world.

The country she now found herself in was called the Great Zhou Dynasty, and its capital was the Capital City. The era she had transmigrated into bore no resemblance to any period in her knowledge of history — this was clearly a fictional ancient world.

Prince Yu, Xiao Yu, was the emperor's own younger brother. His fiefdom lay in Yuzhou on the northwestern frontier. For his outstanding military service, he had been granted the title of "Single-Character Equal-Rank Prince," a rank second only to the Crown Prince.

But precisely because of this, he had also become a thorn in certain people's sides.

It was said that the emperor both feared and trusted him, and that the various factions at court watched him like hawks. The prince's residence appeared grand and glorious, but beneath the surface, danger lurked on every side.

When Su Nian heard all this, she could not help feeling a measure of curiosity about Prince Yu.

To stand unshaken in such a treacherous arena of power — this prince must be no ordinary man.

"By the way," she asked Xiao Cui, "what is my current status here? What does the prince plan to do with me?"

Xiao Cui hemmed and hawed. "Well... Xiao Cui isn't entirely sure either. But His Highness said you are a guest, and that you'll be staying at the residence for a while."

A guest? Su Nian's feelings were complicated.

She was a girl of unknown origins, rescued by the prince and now living in his household. If word got out, she couldn't imagine what kind of gossip would spread.

Besides, she still needed to figure out why those men in black had been hunting her. Who had the original owner of this body offended?

Su Nian lay under the quilt, her thoughts racing.

In her previous life, she had been an orphan, raised in a welfare institution. After growing up, she had worked hard, scrimped and saved, and her greatest wish had been to buy a home of her own.

Instead of buying that home, she had transmigrated.

She wondered how her original body was doing. Would her colleagues notice she was missing? Would her company file a police report?

The thought made her smile bitterly.

Enough — dwelling on it was useless. Since she had transmigrated, she would simply have to make the best of this new life.

In this lifetime, she would not live as exhaustingly as she had in her last.

She would live for herself, for once.

On the seventh day of her recovery, Su Nian was finally able to get out of bed.

She had Xiao Cui help her walk around the courtyard so she could familiarize herself with the surroundings.

Prince Yu's Residence was enormous, covering well over a hundred *mu* of land. The small courtyard where they stayed alone had a front yard, a rear yard, a garden, and a lotus pond.

"Miss, this is our courtyard — it's called the Listening Rain Pavilion." Xiao Cui pointed things out as they walked. "His Highness specially had it renovated for you, and even assigned maids and matrons to attend to you. The courtyard isn't large, but it's very peaceful."

Su Nian nodded, her gaze sweeping her surroundings.

The courtyard was not large, but it was immaculately kept. Orchids grew in the corners, and beneath the window sat a stone table with several stone benches. Sunlight filtered through the gaps in the leaves, casting dappled shadows — it was actually rather pleasant.

If she ignored her current predicament, it would make a rather nice place to spend one's later years.

Just then, footsteps sounded at the courtyard gate.

Su Nian turned to look and saw a tall, upright man in a dark brocade robe striding in.

He was over one hundred and eighty-five centimeters tall, his ink-black hair bound at the crown and secured with a jade hairpin. His features were deep and striking — sword-like brows that reached his temples, a high-bridged nose, and thin lips pressed firmly together.

He wore a dark brocade robe with subtle patterns, a jade belt at his waist, and black boots on his feet. His entire being radiated an imposing, frosty aura that made one afraid to meet his gaze.

Su Nian's heart skipped a beat.

This must be Prince Yu — Xiao Yu.

Just as the rumors said: cold in face and cold in heart. Merely standing there, he exuded an overwhelming sense of pressure.

Xiao Yu's gaze lingered on her for a moment, as though appraising her.

Su Nian grew uncomfortable under his scrutiny and instinctively lowered her eyes.

She did not know what to say. Though he was her savior, she was, after all, a transmigrator who knew nothing of this world. Say too much and she risked exposing herself; say nothing and she would seem rude.

Just as she was wrestling with the dilemma, a low voice spoke:

"Are your injuries healing?"

Su Nian was taken aback — she hadn't expected him to initiate conversation.

"Thank you for saving my life, Your Highness." She curtsied, striving to keep her tone natural. "I am much better now."

Xiao Yu said nothing. He simply looked at her, his expression unreadable.

That gaze was like an X-ray, as though he could see right through her.

Su Nian felt a prickle of unease. Had she given something away?

After a long moment, Xiao Yu finally withdrew his gaze. "Since your injuries have improved, come to my study tomorrow. There are some matters I wish to ask you."

With that, he turned and walked away, giving her no opportunity to refuse.

Su Nian frowned slightly as she watched him go.

Matters to ask? About what?

Had he realized she had lost her memory? Or had he noticed something else?

A vague unease settled in her chest.

"Miss," Xiao Cui whispered, leaning close. "His Highness is so scary! Xiao Cui didn't even dare look at him!"

Su Nian gave a wry smile. "He made me nervous too."

But now that she thought about it, Prince Yu really was remarkably handsome. Cold, yes — but with exquisite features and an extraordinary presence. In the modern world, he would have been the textbook definition of an aloof heartthrob.

Pity he was an ancient man.

Su Nian shook her head and banished the wayward thoughts.

The most important thing now was to figure out what Xiao Yu wanted to ask. She needed to prepare carefully and not reveal any cracks in her story.
