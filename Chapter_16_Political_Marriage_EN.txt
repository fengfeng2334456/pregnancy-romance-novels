## Chapter 16: Political Marriage

The good times did not last.

After the palace banquet incident, although Xiao Yu had cleared his name, the political landscape at court grew even more complex.

The Emperor was furious that a Northern Di prince had infiltrated the Capital City. He ordered a thorough investigation of all suspicious individuals at court, and for a time, everyone was on edge.

Meanwhile, Consort De — due to her connection with the Northern Di prince (Senior Matron Cui was her trusted aide) — had been confined to the palace by the Emperor and forbidden to leave.

Though Xiao Yu was not implicated, his position had actually become more difficult.

Some at court claimed that Prince Yu's achievements overshadowed the Emperor's. Others said he had colluded with Northern Di. Still others accused him of plotting rebellion...

Rumors flew everywhere, leaving him overwhelmed.

Worse still, the Emperor himself seemed to have grown wary of him.

That day, Xiao Yu came to Su Nian's courtyard, his expression grave.

Su Nian saw him and her heart skipped a beat.

With difficulty, she rose from her chair. One hand supporting her waist, the other cradling her already noticeably protruding belly. She was in her second trimester, and her body felt increasingly heavy. Even a simple movement to stand required great effort.

"What is it?" she asked. "What happened?"

Xiao Yu was silent for a long while before speaking:

"His Majesty has commanded me to take a wife."

Su Nian's heart plummeted.

A commanded marriage?

A marriage for Xiao Yu?

"Who... who is the bride?" she asked, her voice strained.

Xiao Yu looked at her, a flash of pain in his eyes.

"The daughter of the Chancellor's household — Liu Ru Mei."

Liu Ru Mei. Su Nian remembered the name.

She was Liu Ru Yan's elder sister, the most talented woman in the Capital City, and it was said that she had admired Xiao Yu for years.

Moreover, her father was the current Chancellor — a family of great prominence, well-matched in status with Prince Yu's Residence.

Su Nian's face went pale.

She had known this day would come eventually.

Xiao Yu was a prince — how could he possibly marry a nameless orphan girl of unknown origins?

Even if he liked her, it could not withstand the cruelty of reality.

"Then... what did you say?" she asked.

Xiao Yu grasped her hand. "I refused."

Su Nian's heart leaped. "What did His Majesty say?"

Xiao Yu gave a bitter smile. "His Majesty flew into a rage. He said that by defying his command, I was being disloyal and unfilial."

Su Nian's heart sank again.

Of course...

"Your Highness," she took a deep breath, "you do not need to offend His Majesty on my account."

Xiao Yu looked at her. "I will not."

Su Nian shook her head. "Your Highness, you are a prince of the realm. You cannot jeopardize the greater scheme of things for the sake of one woman."

Xiao Yu frowned. "What do you mean?"

Su Nian lowered her head, not daring to meet his eyes.

"I... I have thought about it," her voice trembled slightly. "I cannot drag you down. If you marry the lady of the Liu family, it would be of great benefit to your political future."

Xiao Yu's expression darkened.

"Su Nian," his voice grew colder, "are you asking me to marry another woman?"

Su Nian bit her lip. "I don't want that. But... but I have no choice."

She raised her head, tears pooling in her eyes.

"I am a transmigrator. I have no identity, no way to help you. I don't even know why I came to this world..."

Her voice grew lower and lower, until it was barely audible.

"I only bring you trouble. It would be better to—"

"Enough!" Xiao Yu cut her off, his voice tight with suppressed fury.

Su Nian startled and looked up at him.

Xiao Yu took a deep breath, forcing down his anger.

"Su Nian, I am going to ask you one question, and I want you to answer honestly."

Su Nian nodded.

Xiao Yu looked at her, enunciating each word carefully: "Do you want me to marry someone else?"

Su Nian froze.

Did she... want that?

The answer, of course, was no.

She loved Xiao Yu. She did not want any other woman near him.

But what right did she have to stop him?

"N... no," she said at last, honestly.

Xiao Yu's gaze softened.

"Then that is enough," he said. "I will not marry anyone else."

Su Nian shook her head. "But what about His Majesty?"

Xiao Yu fell silent for a moment. "I will find a way."

Su Nian looked at him, not knowing what to say.

She knew that when he said "find a way," it would likely come at a great cost.

---

Three days later, to Su Nian's surprise, Liu Ru Mei actually came to visit in person.

With her belly now over five months along and protruding noticeably, Su Nian made her way to the main hall with great difficulty, supported by a maid. She wore a loose gown, but it still couldn't hide her prominently rounded belly.

Liu Ru Mei sat poised and elegant in a chair, dressed in an exquisite brocade gown, her demeanor refined and graceful. The moment she saw Su Nian, her gaze immediately fell upon Su Nian's belly. A flash of surprise crossed her eyes, quickly replaced by thick mockery.

"So this is Miss Su?" Liu Ru Mei's red lips parted slightly, her voice gentle yet barbed. "She truly is... special, just as the rumors say."

Su Nian supported her waist and slowly sat down. "Lady Liu is too kind."

"Miss Su's belly must be around five or six months along, yes?" Liu Ru Mei's gaze swept over Su Nian's midsection, her tone dripping with contempt. "You must be so tired, carrying a child while living in this residence."

Su Nian unconsciously cradled her belly. "Thank you for your concern, Lady Liu."

"Concern?" Liu Ru Mei let out a light laugh. "Miss Su has misunderstood. I simply felt that your appearance... was quite an eyesore."

She stood and circled Su Nian, examining Su Nian as one would examine goods.

"Look at you — your belly is as big as a wok, you need help just to walk. Where is the composure befitting a woman?" Liu Ru Mei covered her mouth with a sleeve and laughed. "What kind of person is His Highness? How could he be weighed down by someone like you?"

Su Nian's face turned pale, her hands gripping the armrest tightly.

"Lady Liu," she forced herself to remain composed, "my affairs with His Highness are none of your concern."

"None of my concern?" Liu Ru Mei said coldly. "I am merely giving you some friendly advice. Look at your current state — you can barely even stand. And yet you still try to monopolize His Highness? Once I enter the residence, there will be no place for you in this household."

She leaned close to Su Nian and lowered her voice. "Did you think that bearing a child could keep His Highness? How naive. When this child is born, whether it's a boy or a girl, it's merely illegitimate."

Su Nian bit her lip, her eyes reddening.

She wanted to argue, but found she had nothing to say.

Liu Ru Mei was right. In her current state, she truly was a sorry sight. Her big belly made movement difficult, and even walking required great care. How could she compare to Liu Ru Mei's elegance and poise?

"Have Lady Liu said her piece?" Su Nian held back her tears with effort. "If so, please take your leave. I am unwell and cannot see you out."

Liu Ru Mei looked at her pathetic state and smiled with satisfaction. "Miss Su should rest well. Don't overexert yourself. After all, this child is your only bargaining chip."

With that, she turned and left, leaving Su Nian sitting alone in the chair, her hands cradling her belly, tears finally spilling over.
