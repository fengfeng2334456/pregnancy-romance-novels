## Chapter 1: Accidental Transmigration

*"It hurts..."*

This was Su Nian's first sensation upon regaining consciousness.

Her head felt as if it had been struck by a hammer, a dull, throbbing pain. She wanted to reach up and touch it, but her arms were so weak she could barely lift them.

What was happening?

She clearly remembered working overtime on a project, still at the company at two in the morning staring at the computer. Suddenly, everything went black, and then she knew nothing more.

Could she have died from overwork?

No, this pain was too real; it didn't feel like a dream.

Su Nian forced her eyes open. What greeted her was an unfamiliar, ancient-style room.

A carved wooden bed, light blue gauze curtains, and a faint scent of sandalwood in the air. She lay on a hard bed, covered by a thin quilt embroidered with mandarin ducks.

Where was this? A hospital? No, how could a hospital have a place like this?

She struggled to sit up and looked around. The room wasn't large, but it was elegantly arranged. On the dressing table sat a bronze mirror and some jars and bottles she didn't recognize. The window was papered, and outside, she could vaguely see the outline of pavilions and towers.

"What... what is this situation?"

Su Nian was completely bewildered.

She was clearly an ordinary white-collar worker in the 21st century, living in Beijing's Chaoyang District, working nine to six with overtime every day, living a two-point life between home and work. How could she wake up in a place that only existed in ancient dramas?

Could it be a prank by a friend?

No, she didn't have any friends who would do such a boring thing. Besides, the texture, the smell, the light... everything was too real.

"Miss, you're awake?"

A crisp voice came from the doorway.

Su Nian turned to see a young girl in ancient costume entering with a bowl of medicine. The girl looked about fifteen or sixteen, with a round face and almond-shaped eyes, appearing clever and cute.

Ancient costume? Filming a drama?

Su Nian was even more confused.

"Miss, don't move. Drink the medicine first." The little maid carefully helped her sit up. "The doctor said you were injured on the head and need to rest well."

"I..." Su Nian opened her mouth, her voice hoarse. "Where am I?"

The little maid's face showed surprise: "Miss, don't you remember? This is the Yu Prince's Residence! Three days ago, you were found in a dilapidated temple outside the city, covered in injuries and unconscious. It was our prince who passed by and brought you back for treatment."

Yu Prince's Residence? Prince?

Su Nian felt her mind was in complete chaos.

"I... what's my name?" she asked tentatively.

The little maid was stunned, then her eyes reddened: "Miss, what's wrong with you? Your name is Su Nian! Although Miss doesn't remember, Xiao Cui always remembers your name! Miss is a good person. If you hadn't saved me that day, I would have been beaten to death by those human traffickers..."

As she spoke, the little maid actually started wiping her tears.

Su Nian... Su Nian. The name was the same as hers.

No, what was going on?

She took a deep breath, forcing herself to calm down: "Um... Xiao Cui, right? Can you tell me, what dynasty is this?"

The little maid was confused by her question: "Dynasty? What does Miss mean?"

"I mean, what year is it now? Who is the emperor?"

"The twentieth year of Tianyou. The current sage is the third son of the previous emperor..." The little maid paused, suddenly realizing, "Miss, how can you not even know these things? Could you have lost your memory?"

Su Nian didn't answer her question because she was completely shocked.

The twentieth year of Tianyou? Previous emperor? Third prince?

This was... ancient times? She was in ancient times?

Su Nian looked down at her hands—slender, fair, with neatly trimmed nails, but with a thin layer of calluses on her palms. These weren't her hands. Her hands were also fair, but her palms were smooth.

She suddenly threw off the quilt and looked down—

This wasn't her body.

She was a twenty-eight-year-old urban white-collar worker, 165 cm tall, weighing a little over 100 jin. But the body before her was small and thin, apparently not even 160 cm, and clearly still in its developmental stage.

"Heavens, are you playing a joke on me?"

Su Nian muttered to herself, feeling as if the sky was about to collapse.

She had transmigrated! She had actually transmigrated!

And she had transmigrated to a strange ancient dynasty, becoming a young girl of unknown identity.

This was too absurd, wasn't it?

"Miss? Miss, what's wrong with you?" Xiao Cui saw her pale face and hurriedly came forward to support her. "Miss, don't scare Xiao Cui!"

Su Nian took a deep breath, forcing herself to calm down.

No, she couldn't panic. The more critical the moment, the more she needed to stay calm.

Since she had already transmigrated, she had to accept reality. Now the most important thing was to figure out the identity of this body and why she was injured.

"Xiao Cui," she lowered her voice, trying to keep her tone steady, "let me ask you, what did I do before? Why was I injured?"

Xiao Cui wiped away her tears and answered seriously: "Miss was from a farming family outside the city. Your parents died early, and you were alone. Three days ago, Xiao Cui was chased by human traffickers and fled to the dilapidated temple to hide, where I met Miss. Miss saved Xiao Cui and took me in. But later..."

She paused, her voice dropping: "Later, a group of men in black came to assassinate Miss. Miss protected Xiao Cui and was knocked unconscious. If the prince hadn't happened to pass by, Miss might have..."

Speaking of this, Xiao Cui's eyes reddened again.

Su Nian frowned.

The original owner of this body was being hunted? Why would they hunt her? She was from a farming family; she shouldn't have any enemies.

Could it be that this body was hiding some secret?

"What about those men in black? Were they caught?"

"I don't know. The prince sent people to investigate, but it seems they didn't find anything."

Su Nian was silent for a moment, feeling a vague unease in her heart.

It seemed the identity of this body was not that simple. She had to be careful and not reveal any flaws.

"Xiao Cui, I've lost my memory now and don't remember anything. Can you tell me about the situation here? Especially about Prince Yu... what kind of person is he?"

Hearing this, Xiao Cui's eyes lit up: "The prince! The prince is the God of War of the Great Zhou Dynasty! Young and with illustrious military achievements, he killed the Beidi people until they trembled with fear! In the entire capital, there's no one who isn't afraid of the prince!"

Speaking of this, she lowered her voice again: "But the prince's personality is a bit cold, and he doesn't like to talk much. But the prince is a good person! If the prince hadn't saved Miss, Miss would have died! Xiao Cui thinks the prince is the best, best person in the world!"

Su Nian listened, feeling slightly relieved.

It sounded like this Prince Yu should be a positive character, at least not someone who would kill indiscriminately. Since she was now in the prince's residence, she should be safe for the time being.

But she still had to figure out the identity of this body as soon as possible, and why those men in black were hunting her.

Also, what exactly was this world? Could she go back?

A series of questions swirled in her mind.

But for now, she could only recover from her injuries first, then think of a way.

"Xiao Cui, give me the medicine." She reached out her hand.

Xiao Cui hurriedly handed over the medicine bowl: "Miss, drink it while it's hot."

Su Nian took the medicine bowl, took a deep breath, and drank it all in one go.

Bitter! So bitter she frowned!

But she didn't complain, just silently swallowed.

This was her first bowl of medicine after transmigrating to ancient times.

There would probably be many more bitter days to come.

After drinking the medicine, Xiao Cui brought another bowl of porridge. Su Nian was starving and finished it in a few gulps.

After eating, she felt a bit stronger.

"Where is the prince now? I want to thank him in person."

"The prince is busy with affairs and rarely in the residence." Xiao Cui said, "But the prince instructed that Miss should rest assured in the residence to recover. When you're better, you can make plans."

Su Nian nodded, but her mind was calculating.

It seemed she would have to stay in this Yu Prince's Residence for the short term. A transmigrator, in an unfamiliar ancient world, without any identity proof, even eating was a problem.

The prince's residence, at least, was a safe place to stay.

As for what to do in the future... she could only take it one step at a time.
