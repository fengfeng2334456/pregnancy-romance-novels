## Chapter 22: Difficult Childbirth

Xiao Yu had found Su Nian, but he did not take her away.

Because he knew she was still angry.

He explained to her what had happened that day.

Everything Liu Ru Yan had told her that day had been lies.

He had indeed agreed to a condition set by the Emperor, but the condition was not to send Su Nian away — it was to send Liu Ru Yan away.

Liu Ru Yan had spread rumors and framed Su Nian; he would no longer tolerate her.

And the words Su Nian had overheard about "sending her away" had been deliberately fabricated by Liu Ru Yan to deceive her.

Su Nian listened, and tears streamed down her face.

So... so she had misunderstood.

She had thought Xiao Yu no longer wanted her, and that was why she had left.

But the truth was, Xiao Yu had never once considered abandoning her.

"I'm sorry," she sobbed. "It was my fault. I shouldn't have doubted you."

Xiao Yu pulled her into his arms, gently stroking her hair.

"Don't blame yourself. I didn't explain things clearly to you," he said. "From now on, no matter what happens, I will tell you everything. Don't let your imagination run wild on your own."

Su Nian nodded, leaning against his chest.

At this moment, she felt like the happiest person in the world.

---

Xiao Yu did not take Su Nian away.

Because she was about to give birth and was in no condition to travel long distances.

He stayed behind, remaining by her side.

The villagers were astonished — they had never expected Prince Yu to come to their little village in person.

But they asked no questions, quietly accepting this distinguished guest.

---

Days passed, and Su Nian's belly grew larger and larger.

She was already over nine months along and could give birth at any moment.

That night, Su Nian suddenly felt a sharp pain in her belly.

"Ah..." She clutched her belly, waking from her sleep.

Xiao Yu immediately sat up. "What's wrong?"

"My belly... my belly hurts..." Su Nian bit her lip, her forehead covered in cold sweat.

Xiao Yu quickly lit a candle. Seeing her pale face, his heart tightened.

"Is it time?" he asked.

"I... I don't know..." Su Nian shook her head. "It just... just hurts..."

Before she could finish speaking, another sharp pain came from her belly, like someone twisting her intestines with a knife.

"Ah!" She couldn't help but cry out, her hands clutching the bedsheets tightly, her knuckles turning white.

Xiao Yu was terrified and shouted, "Someone! Call the midwife!"

---

The midwife arrived and examined her. "It's still early. The cervix hasn't fully dilated yet."

"Then why does she hurt so much?" Xiao Yu asked anxiously.

"These are labor pains," the midwife said. "How can giving birth not hurt? Let her endure it. She can only give birth when the cervix is fully dilated."

Su Nian lay in bed, one hand clutching Xiao Yu's hand tightly, the other covering her belly, trembling from the pain.

The contractions came one after another, becoming more frequent and more intense.

She felt as if her belly was being constantly hammered, each contraction nearly causing her to faint.

"Xiao Yu..." she choked out. "I... I hurt so much..."

"I know, I know..." Xiao Yu held her hand tightly, his eyes reddening. "Su Nian, endure it a little longer. It will be over soon..."

"I... I can't endure it..." Su Nian cried. "It hurts too much..."

She had never imagined that giving birth would hurt so much.

That pain was like someone tearing her body in half, like someone churning her belly into a stormy sea.

She felt her consciousness blurring, her vision going dark.

---

The contractions lasted the entire night.

Su Nian was in agony, her clothes soaked with sweat, her hair sticking to her face.

She moaned constantly, her hands clutching the bedsheets tightly, her knuckles turning white.

"Ah!" Another intense contraction made her scream.

The baby in her belly seemed to be protesting, kicking and thrashing, making her even more miserable.

"Little one..." she cried. "You... come out quickly... Mother... Mother can't bear it anymore..."

Xiao Yu watched her suffering, his heart aching as if being cut by a knife.

He wished he could endure all of this for her, but there was nothing he could do.

"Su Nian," he held her hand tightly. "Hold on a little longer. It will be over soon..."

"I... I really can't..." Su Nian said weakly. "I... I have no strength..."

Her face was deathly pale, her lips cracked, her whole body as if fished out of water.

Xiao Yu looked at her, tears streaming down his face.

"Su Nian," he choked out. "I'm sorry... it's all my fault... for making you suffer like this..."

Su Nian shook her head, barely managing a smile. "No... it's not your fault... this... this is what I wanted..."

Before she could finish speaking, another intense contraction hit.

"Ah!" She screamed, her whole body trembling.

---

By dawn, the cervix was finally fully dilated.

The midwife said, "It's time to give birth."

Su Nian was helped onto the birthing bed, her legs spread, positioned properly.

"Push!" the midwife shouted. "Follow my rhythm. Push!"

Su Nian gritted her teeth, her hands clutching the edge of the bed, using all her strength to push down.

"Ah!" She screamed, feeling as if her lower body was being torn apart.

The baby in her belly was stuck there, unable to move forward or back.

"Push harder!" the midwife shouted. "I can see the head! Push harder!"

Su Nian pushed again. This time, she felt her consciousness blurring.

That pain had exceeded her tolerance.

"I... I can't..." she said weakly. "I... I have no strength..."

"No!" the midwife said sternly. "The baby is stuck. If you don't push harder, the baby will suffocate!"

Su Nian was startled when she heard this.

She couldn't let her baby be in danger.

She gritted her teeth and pushed again.

"Ah!" She screamed, feeling as if her body was being torn in half.

---

Xiao Yu stood outside the door, listening to Su Nian's screams inside, his heart aching as if being cut by a knife.

He wished he could rush in, but he knew he couldn't help.

"Su Nian..." he whispered. "You must hold on..."

Time passed second by second, and Su Nian's screams grew weaker.

Xiao Yu's heart was in his throat.

"Why isn't it over yet?" he asked anxiously.

"Almost, almost," the midwife's voice came from inside. "Push harder!"

Another shrill scream, and then, a loud baby's cry.

"It's born! It's born!" the midwife shouted. "It's a boy!"

Xiao Yu's heart finally relaxed.

He pushed open the door and rushed in.

Su Nian lay in bed, her face deathly pale, covered in sweat, but with a happy smile on her face.

The midwife held a swaddled baby and handed it to Xiao Yu. "Congratulations, Your Highness. It's a little prince."

Xiao Yu took the baby, looking at that wrinkled little face, his eyes reddening.

"Su Nian," he walked to the bedside and held her hand. "Thank you."

Su Nian smiled weakly. "Give the child... a name..."

Xiao Yu thought for a moment. "Let's call him Xiao Nian. Nian, as in longing and remembering. To remind me never to forget you."

Su Nian's tears fell again.

Xiao Nian.

The child of Xiao Yu and Su Nian.

What a beautiful name.

---

Su Nian's delivery was very difficult.

She experienced a full day and night of labor pains before giving birth to the child.

But her body had suffered great damage because of this and needed proper recuperation.

Xiao Yu stayed by her side, caring for her daily needs without leaving.

"Su Nian," he held a bowl of chicken soup and fed it to her lips. "Drink some soup to nourish your body."

Su Nian opened her mouth and took a sip.

"Where is the baby?" she asked.

"The wet nurse is holding him," Xiao Yu said. "You just gave birth and need to rest."

"I want to see him..." Su Nian said.

Xiao Yu nodded and had the wet nurse bring the baby over.

Su Nian took the baby, looking at that sleeping little face, her heart filled with happiness.

"He's so small..." she whispered.

"Yes," Xiao Yu smiled too. "But his cry was very loud. He must be very healthy."

Su Nian lowered her head and kissed the baby's forehead gently.

"Little one," she whispered. "Mother loves you."

Xiao Yu looked at the mother and child, his heart filled with gratitude.

Thank heaven for letting him meet Su Nian.

Thank her for being willing to give birth to this child for him.

He swore that in this life, he would protect them well and never let them suffer any wrong.

---
