## Chapter 18: Rival Schemes

That night, Su Nian did not sleep at all.

She thought about many things.

Was Xiao Yu's kindness to her genuine?

Or was it only because of the child she carried?

If one day the child was born, would he still treat her well?

She did not know.

She only knew that she was afraid.

Afraid that all of this was just a dream. Afraid that Xiao Yu was merely using her. Afraid that she would give everything, only to end up with nothing.

At dawn, she finally made her decision.

Since he was going to send her away, she would leave on her own terms.

Rather than being driven out, she would depart with dignity.

---

Just as Su Nian was preparing to pack her belongings, Liu Ru Yan arrived again.

"Miss Su," she walked into the courtyard with a smile. "The weather is lovely today. I specially came to see you."

Su Nian rose from her chair with her prominent belly. "What brings Concubine Liu here?"

Liu Ru Yan's gaze fell upon Su Nian's protruding belly, a flash of cruelty flickering in her eyes.

"Miss Su, staying in the courtyard all day isn't good for the baby." Liu Ru Yan said insincerely. "I've specially ordered the kitchen to prepare some tonic and had the garden tidied up. Why not take a stroll?"

Su Nian looked at her warily. "Thank you for your kindness, Concubine Liu, but I'm not feeling well and don't wish to go out."

"Not feeling well?" Liu Ru Yan said coldly. "Miss Su is being too narrow-minded, aren't you?"

"I wouldn't dare."

"If you dare, then come along." Liu Ru Yan grabbed Su Nian's hand and pulled her toward the door.

Su Nian, with her six-month belly, stumbled from the tug and nearly fell.

"Concubine Liu!" She cried out, hastily gripping the door frame and cradling her belly. "Please... please go slowly. I... I can't walk fast."

Liu Ru Yan glanced back at her, eyes full of mockery. "Miss Su's body is truly delicate."

She slowed her pace, but Su Nian still struggled.

Her belly was heavy and cumbersome, each step requiring tremendous effort. She supported her waist with one hand and cradled her belly with the other. Beads of sweat soon appeared on her forehead.

"Concubine Liu," she breathed heavily. "Could... could we rest for a moment?"

"Rest what?" Liu Ru Yan said impatiently. "We're almost there."

She led Su Nian to the garden and pointed at the scattered leaves on the ground. "Miss Su, look at how messy this garden is. The maids aren't being diligent. Why don't you help tidy up?"

Su Nian stared. "Me?"

"Yes," Liu Ru Yan said venomously. "Since you have nothing to do all day anyway, you might as well get some exercise. It's good for the baby."

"But..." Su Nian looked at her belly. "I... I can't bend down..."

"Can't bend?" Liu Ru Yan said coldly. "Miss Su is unwilling to help me, then?"

"I'm not, I..."

"Then start." Liu Ru Yan interrupted her. "Sweep all these leaves over there."

Su Nian bit her lip and looked at the scattered leaves, her heart full of grievance and helplessness.

She knew Liu Ru Yan was deliberately making things difficult for her, but she had no way to refuse.

She picked up the broom and tried to bend down with difficulty.

Her belly was in the way, preventing her from bending. She could only squat partially, one hand supporting her belly, the other awkwardly sweeping the leaves.

Before sweeping a few times, she was already out of breath, her back aching so badly she couldn't straighten up.

"Concubine Liu," she supported her waist and said with difficulty. "I... I really can't..."

"Can't?" Liu Ru Yan sat leisurely in the pavilion, sipping tea. "Miss Su has only swept a few times and is already complaining? How pampered."

She put down her teacup and walked over slowly. "Since you can't sweep, then draw water to water the flowers."

She pointed at the well nearby. "Go. Draw a bucket of water."

Su Nian looked at the well, her face pale.

She could barely walk now, let alone draw water.

"Concubine Liu," she pleaded. "I... I really can't do it..."

"Can't?" Liu Ru Yan said coldly. "Miss Su, are you defying my orders?"

"I wouldn't dare..."

"Then go!"

Su Nian bit her lip and walked toward the well step by step.

Her belly was so big that her center of gravity was off balance when walking. Every step required caution. She supported her waist with one hand and cradled her belly with the other. Sweat covered her entire body.

When she finally reached the well, she looked at the heavy wooden bucket, despair rising in her heart.

She bent down, trying to pick up the bucket, but her belly was in the way, preventing her from bending.

She could only kneel on one knee, one hand bracing the ground, the other reaching for the bucket.

"Ah!" Her foot slipped, and she fell forward.

At the critical moment, she instinctively braced herself with her hand and protected her belly.

But her wrist was sprained, pain causing tears to stream down her face.

"Concubine Liu!" She lay on the ground and cried out. "Please... please let me go... I... I really can't..."

Liu Ru Yan walked over and looked down at her pathetic state, a cold smile on her face.

"Miss Su, what happened?" She said hypocritically. "Why did you fall?"

"I... my stomach hurts..." Su Nian lay on the ground, one hand covering her belly, her face deathly pale.

A sharp pain shot through her belly, causing her whole body to tremble.

Liu Ru Yan looked at her painful expression, a flash of satisfaction in her eyes.

"Stomach pain?" She said coldly. "Miss Su must be faking illness, then?"

"No... not..." Su Nian couldn't speak from the pain. "Please... please call a doctor..."

"Call a doctor?" Liu Ru Yan squatted down and leaned close to her ear, lowering her voice. "Su Nian, who do you think you are? Carrying His Highness's child makes you think you're great? I tell you, whether this child survives or not is still uncertain."

Su Nian's eyes widened in horror.

"You... what do you want to do?"

"What do I want to do?" Liu Ru Yan stood up and said coldly. "I simply want you to understand that in this residence, you don't get to act arbitrarily."

She turned and left, leaving Su Nian alone on the ground, her belly aching so badly that cold sweat poured down her body.

---

After a while, Xiao Cui finally noticed Su Nian was missing and searched everywhere. She finally found her in the garden.

"Miss!" Xiao Cui exclaimed and rushed over to help her up. "What happened to you?"

"Xiao Cui..." Su Nian said weakly. "My... my stomach hurts..."

Xiao Cui saw her pale face and was terrified. "Miss, I'll help you back and call the doctor right away!"

She helped Su Nian up with great difficulty. Su Nian, with her prominent belly, was completely powerless and almost entirely relied on Xiao Cui for support.

"Be careful..." Su Nian bit her lip. "I... I can't walk..."

Xiao Cui half-carried and half-supported her back to her room.

Su Nian lay on her side in bed, one hand clutching the bedsheets tightly, the other cradling her belly. Her forehead was covered in cold sweat.

"Little one..." she murmured. "Mother is sorry... Mother didn't protect you well..."

A wave of sharp pain after another came from her belly, nearly causing her to faint.

The doctor arrived and, after taking her pulse, said she had experienced fetal distress and needed bed rest. She absolutely could not overwork herself again.

Su Nian lay in bed, tears silently streaming down her face.

She touched her belly, fear and despair filling her heart.

If this continued, could her child still be saved?

---

That night, Su Nian lay in bed, tossing and turning.

Her big belly pressed on her, making it hard to breathe. Her waist ached terribly. She lay on her side, one hand propping herself up on the bed, the other gently stroking her belly.

"Little one," she whispered. "Mother is sorry... Mother was useless and couldn't protect you..."

A gentle fetal movement came from within, as if comforting her.

Su Nian's tears fell again.

She couldn't go on like this.

She had to leave.

Even with her big belly, even with a difficult road ahead, she had to leave this wretched place.

She couldn't let her child be harmed in any way.

---

At dawn, she finally made her decision.

She packed her belongings, left a letter, and quietly slipped out of the residence.

The letter read:

"Xiao Yu, I have left. I will take good care of the child. You need not worry."

That was all. No farewell, no explanation.

She was afraid that if she wrote even one more word, she would not be able to hold back her tears.

---

By the time Xiao Yu discovered Su Nian was gone, it was already noon.

He rushed into her room and saw the empty space. He stood there, stunned.

"Where is she?" He grabbed Xiao Cui and demanded harshly, "Where is Su Nian?"

Xiao Cui was sobbing. "Miss... Miss has gone! She left a letter and just left!"

Xiao Yu snatched the letter. After reading it, his face turned livid.

"Damn it!" He crumpled the letter into a ball. "Who told her I was going to send her away?!"

Xiao Cui stammered through her tears. "It... it was Concubine Liu. Concubine Liu came yesterday and said... said something about His Majesty's marriage decree, and that His Highness was going to send Miss away..."

A murderous glint flashed in Xiao Yu's eyes.

Liu Ru Yan!

Her again!

He spun around, about to go confront Liu Ru Yan, but the steward stopped him.

"Your Highness, please calm yourself! The most important thing right now is to find Miss Su! Miss Su is pregnant — it is far too dangerous for her to be out there alone!"

Xiao Yu forced himself to calm down.

The steward was right. Now was not the time to assign blame.

He took a deep breath and issued his orders. "Send men out to search immediately! Turn the entire Capital City upside down if you must — but find her!"

"Yes!"

Xiao Yu stood in the empty room, feeling as though something had been hollowed out of his chest.

Su Nian...

How could she do this?

He had told her he would never let her suffer. He had told her he liked her.

Why did she not trust him?

Why did she leave?

He did not know.

He only knew that he could not lose her.
